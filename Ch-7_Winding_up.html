Framework 01

Jurisdictional Clarity Framework — What Counts as "Winding Up"?

§ 2(94A) — Statutory Definition
"Winding Up" = either of two tracks:
  • Track A: Winding up under Companies Act, 2013 (Chapter XX)
  • Track B: Liquidation under Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code, 2016 (IBC)

Note: "Inability to pay debts" as a standalone ground stands omitted post-IBC. IBC now governs that route.

Modes — § 270
Only one active mode remains under CA 2013:
  • Part I (§§ 271–303): Winding Up by the Tribunal (NCLT)
  • Part II (§§ 304–323): Voluntary winding up — OMITTED — now §59 IBC
  • Part III (§§ 324–358): Provisions applicable to every mode
FR Link Liquidation triggers derecognition of assets/liabilities; going-concern assumption collapses → Ind AS 1 disclosure of material uncertainty
Audit Link SA 570 (Going Concern) — auditor must evaluate winding-up petition as an indicator of going-concern doubt; report under CARO 2020 (clause on defaults)
DT/IDT Link On liquidation, capital gains may arise on distribution of assets in excess of cost; IBC resolution plan can carry-forward losses subject to S.79A conditions
Framework 02

Grounds & Petition Framework — §§ 271–272

Ground (§271) Context / Trigger Who Can Petition (§272) Strategic Note
(a) Special Resolution Members voluntarily choose tribunal winding-up Company; Contributories; Registrar; CG/SG authorised person Company cannot petition alone if no SR passed
(b) National Interest Acts against sovereignty, integrity, security, public order, decency Only CG or SG for this ground (§272(f)) Exclusive CG/SG standing for this ground
(c) Fraud / Misfeasance Registrar or CG-authorised person finds fraudulent conduct/formation Registrar (with prior CG sanction); CG-authorised person Company must be given reasonable opportunity before CG sanctions RoC
(d) Default in Filing — 5 years Non-filing of FS or Annual Returns for 5 consecutive preceding FYs Registrar; Any person authorised by CG Exam Trap: 4 years NOT sufficient — must be FIVE
(e) Just & Equitable Deadlock, fraud, loss of substratum, oppression Company; Contributories; Registrar; CG/SG person Tribunal may refuse if other remedy is available & petitioner acts unreasonably
Contributory's Right to Petition — Decision Tree
§ 272(2) — Is a Contributory Eligible to Petition?
IF Shares were originally allotted OR held & registered in name for ≥ 6 months out of 18 months preceding winding-up commencement
THEN Eligible to petition — even if shares fully paid up, even if company has no assets, even if no surplus remains
ELSE Not eligible — unless shares devolved through death of a former holder
NOTE Registrar needs prior CG sanction before presenting petition; company must get reasonable opportunity to represent
Petition Formalities — §272(4) & (5)
Petition by Company:
  • Must be accompanied by Statement of Affairs (prescribed form)
  • Tribunal admits only if statement is attached
Copy to Registrar:
  • A copy must be filed with Registrar
  • Registrar submits views to Tribunal within 60 days
Framework 03

Tribunal Powers & Order Framework — §§ 273–274

§273 — Orders the Tribunal Can Pass
  • Dismiss the petition (with/without costs)
  • Make any interim order
  • Appoint a Provisional Liquidator (until winding-up order)
  • Make a Winding-Up Order (with/without costs)
  • Any other order as it thinks fit

Time Limit: Order must be made within 90 days from date of petition.

Key Rules for Tribunal
  • Cannot refuse winding-up order merely because assets are mortgaged or company has no assets
  • Before appointing Provisional Liquidator → notice to company + reasonable opportunity (unless special written reasons to skip)
  • On just & equitable ground → may refuse if other remedy available & petitioner is acting unreasonably
Context Trigger Point Legal Requirement Strategic Impact
§274 — Statement of Affairs Petition filed by person other than company; prima facie case exists Tribunal directs company to file objections + Statement of Affairs within 30 days (extendable by 30 more days) Failure → company forfeits right to oppose; directors face personal liability
§274(3) — Books to Liquidator Winding-up order passed under §273(1)(d) Directors/officers must submit completed & audited books within 30 days Non-compliance → imprisonment up to 6 months OR fine ₹25,000–₹5 lakh OR both
§274(2) — Penalty Failure to file Statement of Affairs Imprisonment ≤ 6 months / Fine ₹25K–₹5L / Both (directors/officers responsible) Complaint filed before Special Court by Registrar / Liquidator / Tribunal-authorised person
Framework 04

Liquidator Appointment, Removal & Replacement Framework — §§ 275–276

Appointment — §275
  • Appointed by Tribunal at the time of winding-up order
  • Must be an Insolvency Professional registered under IBC 2016
  • Tribunal specifies terms, conditions & fee (based on task, experience, qualification, company size)
  • On appointment → file Declaration within 7 days (conflict of interest / lack of independence)
Provisional Liquidator
  • Tribunal may restrict powers by appointment order or subsequent order
  • Otherwise has same powers as Company Liquidator
  • Can be elevated to Company Liquidator when winding-up order is passed
Removal Grounds — §276
  • Misconduct
  • Fraud or misfeasance
  • Professional incompetence / failure of due care & diligence
  • Inability to act
  • Conflict of interest / lack of independence

Tribunal must give reasonable opportunity of hearing before removal

§276 — What Happens When Liquidator Exits?
IF Liquidator dies / resigns / is removed
THEN Tribunal transfers the work to another Company Liquidator (reasons in writing)
IF Liquidator caused loss/damage to company due to fraud or misfeasance or failure of due care
THEN Tribunal may recover such loss from the liquidator personally + pass other orders
Framework 05

Winding-Up Procedure Framework — §§ 277–284 & §288

1
Tribunal Passes Winding-Up Order → §277
Within 7 days: Tribunal sends intimation to Company Liquidator / PL and Registrar. Registrar endorses records & notifies Official Gazette. For listed companies: Registrar also intimates stock exchanges.
2
Effect: Notice of Discharge → §277(3)
Winding-up order = deemed notice of discharge to all officers, employees, workmen — except when business is continued.
3
Winding-Up Committee Constituted → §277(4) — Within 3 Weeks
Company Liquidator applies to Tribunal for committee comprising: (i) Official Liquidator attached to Tribunal; (ii) Nominee of secured creditors; (iii) Professional nominated by Tribunal. Liquidator is convener.
4
Company Liquidator Submits Report → §281 — Within 60 Days
Report covers: assets (valued by Registered Valuers), capital, liabilities, debts due, guarantees, contributory list, trademarks/IP, subsisting contracts, holding/subsidiary details, legal cases, fraud investigation findings, viability of business.
5
Tribunal Directions on Report → §282
Tribunal fixes time limit for entire proceedings & dissolution. May order sale as going concern. May order fraud investigation (§210). May appoint Sale Committee.
6
Periodical Reports → §288 — Each Quarter
Company Liquidator submits quarterly progress reports. Tribunal may review & modify its own orders on Liquidator's application.
7
Dissolution Order → §302
When affairs completely wound up → Liquidator applies for dissolution. Tribunal orders dissolution. Within 30 days: copy forwarded to Registrar who records the minute of dissolution.
§279 — Stay of Suits on Winding-Up Order — Decision Tree
IF Winding-up order passed OR Provisional Liquidator appointed
THEN No suit / legal proceeding can be commenced or continued against the company without Tribunal's leave (application disposed within 60 days)
EXCEPT Proceedings pending in appeal before Supreme Court or High Court — no stay applies (§279(2))
FR Link Registered Valuer required for asset valuation in Liquidator's report → Ind AS 113 (Fair Value) considerations; impairment writedowns
Audit Link Directors must submit audited books to Liquidator within 30 days → SA 250 (Laws & Regulations); CARO 2020 clause on assets
DT/IDT Link GST implications: Liquidator is a "taxable person" for supply of goods/services during winding-up; needs GST registration (§29 CGST Act)

Contributory & Asset Settlement Framework — §§ 285–287

Scenario Liability to Contribute? Condition
Former member who left No Liability Ceased to be member ≥ 1 year before winding-up commencement
Former member — specific debt No Liability Debt/liability contracted after he ceased to be a member
Former member — present members can't pay Liable When Tribunal finds present members unable to satisfy contributions
Company limited by shares Limited Contribution capped at amount unpaid on shares held
Company limited by guarantee (with share capital) Limited Contribute to extent of sum unpaid on shares held (as if company were limited by shares)
Advisory Committee — §287 (IBS High-Yield)
Composition:
  • Maximum 12 members — creditors + contributories or other persons
  • Chairperson: Company Liquidator (always)
  • Meeting convened within 30 days of winding-up order by Liquidator
Rights & Role:
  • Right to inspect books, assets, properties at reasonable time
  • Advise Company Liquidator on distribution & administration
  • Report to Tribunal on directed matters
⚠️ Exam Trap (Example 2): Advisory Committee of 13 members is INVALID — ceiling is 12. This is a direct examiner favourite.
Framework 07

Payment Priority Framework — §§ 326–327

§326 — Overriding Preferential Payments (Trumps Everything)

These override secured creditors — paid FIRST before anyone else:

(a) Workmen's Dues — paid in priority to ALL other debts
Including wages, holiday remuneration, compensation, PF/pension/gratuity
(b) Pari Passu with Secured Creditor's Shortfall
Where secured creditor couldn't fully realise security → workmen's portion of security ranked equally
Workmen's Portion Formula:
Workmen's Portion = (Value of Security) × [Workmen's Dues ÷ (Workmen's Dues + Debts to Secured Creditors)]

Exam Example: Security = ₹1L | Workmen = ₹1L | Secured Debt = ₹3L → Aggregate = ₹4L → Workmen's Portion = ₹1L × (1L/4L) = ₹25,000
Priority Rank Category (§327) Period / Limit Strategic Impact
1st Workmen's dues (§326) — wages, holiday pay, PF, compensation 2 years before winding-up order (Proviso §326) Paid within 30 days of asset sale; pari passu with secured creditors on security
2nd Government revenues, taxes, cesses, rates Due in 12 months before relevant date Relevant date = date of PL appointment or winding-up order (if no PL)
2nd Wages / salary of employees (not workmen) ≤ 4 months within 12 months before relevant date Statutory cap per employee as notified
2nd ESI contributions, holiday remuneration, compensation, PF/gratuity 12 months before relevant date These debts rank equally & paid in full; abate proportionally if assets insufficient
2nd Expenses of investigation (§§ 213, 216) As applicable Classified as preferential — comes before unsecured creditors
3rd Secured Creditors (on their security) After §326 satisfied Floating charge created within 12 months → invalid unless company was solvent at creation (§332)
4th Unsecured Creditors Pari passu from residue Often recover nil in practice
§327(7) — Inapplicability Note

Sections 326 & 327 do NOT apply to liquidation under the IBC, 2016. IBC has its own waterfall mechanism (§53 IBC). This is a critical distinction for IBS case studies involving IBC resolution vs CA-2013 winding up.

FR Link Preferential creditors create financial liabilities classified under IAS 32 / Ind AS 32 — liquidation value ≠ carrying amount; disclosure of liquidation basis accounting required
Audit Link Auditor verifying adequacy of provision for liquidation expenses; CARO 2020 — clause on dues to employees & PF/ESI
DT/IDT Link Tax dues rank as preferential under §327(a); however IBC §53 overrides and tax authorities rank lower — significant tension; IBC prevails per SC ruling in Ghanashyam Mishra
Framework 08

Avoidance of Transactions Framework — §§ 328–335

Section Transaction Type Time Trigger Effect / Remedy
§328 Fraudulent Preference — putting creditor/surety in better position than they would be in liquidation Within 6 months before winding-up application Tribunal may declare invalid & restore position; SC in IDBI Bank v. Official Liquidator confirmed this
§329 Transfers Not in Good Faith — not in ordinary course of business & not to bona fide purchaser for value Within 1 year before presentation of winding-up petition Void against Company Liquidator
§330 Assignment of all assets to trustees for benefit of all creditors Any time Absolutely void — no time condition
§332 Floating Charge on undertaking/property Created within 12 months preceding commencement of winding up Invalid — unless company was solvent immediately after creation; valid only for fresh cash consideration + 5% p.a. interest
§334 Post-Commencement Disposals — property, share transfers, member status changes After commencement of winding up Void — unless Tribunal orders otherwise
§335 Attachments / Executions / Sales after winding-up commencement (without Tribunal leave) After commencement Void — except proceedings for recovery of tax/dues payable to Government
§328 Fraudulent Preference — Applicability Test (IBS Problem-Solving)
IF Transaction is a transfer of property / delivery of goods / payment / execution
AND IF Done within 6 months before winding-up application
AND IF Puts a creditor / surety / guarantor in a better position than in a normal liquidation
THEN = Fraudulent Preference → Tribunal can declare invalid & restore pre-transaction position
EXAM TIP Descriptive Q1 in this chapter: Sale on Oct 15, 2021 → Winding-up Mar 10, 2022 = within 6 months → §328 applies → Sale declared invalid

Offences & Penalties Framework — §§ 336, 344, 352

Section Offence Who Punishment
§336 Non-disclosure of assets; fraudulent removal/concealment of property (≥₹1,000); falsification of books; false entries; false representations to creditors; fictitious losses Officer of company in liquidation (includes shadow directors) Imprisonment 3–5 years + Fine ₹1L–₹3L. Defence: no intent to defraud
§336(2) Receiving property pawned/pledged knowing it to be improperly disposed Third party receiver Imprisonment 3–5 years + Fine ₹3L–₹5L
§344 Failure to state "Company is being wound up" on every invoice/business letter Company + every officer + Liquidator + receiver/manager who willfully permits Fine ₹50,000–₹3 lakh
§352(8)(a) Retaining undistributed dividends / assets instead of depositing into Company Liquidation Dividend & Undistributed Assets Account Liquidator Interest @ 12% p.a. on retained amount + penalty by Registrar + potential removal by Tribunal
§352 — Company Liquidation Dividend & Undistributed Assets Account — Key Numbers
  • Trigger: dividends unpaid / assets undistributed for 6 months → deposit forthwith into CLDUA Account (scheduled bank)
  • Claimant applies to Registrar (not Tribunal)
  • Registrar must settle within 60 days; else report to Regional Director
  • Unclaimed for 15 years → transferred to General Revenue Account of CG
  • Penalty for non-deposit: 12% p.a. interest (not 15%) Exam Trap — Example 5
  • Tribunal can disallow Liquidator's remuneration + remove from office
Framework 10

Non-Negotiables & IRAC Template

⚡ Compliance Indicators — High-Yield IBS Non-Negotiables

N-015 consecutive FY default (not 4) triggers winding-up ground under §271(d) — a one-year shortfall defeats the entire petition.
N-02Advisory Committee ≤ 12 members — any formation with 13+ is void ab initio. Liquidator chairs it. Must be convened within 30 days.
N-03Fraudulent preference window = 6 months (§328); Good faith transfer void = 1 year (§329); Floating charge void = 12 months (§332) — never confuse these three windows.
N-04Workmen's dues rank pari passu with secured creditors on their security — they do NOT merely rank second; the security itself is subject to workmen's portion charge.
N-05Liquidator's declaration must be filed within 7 days of appointment and continues throughout the appointment — obligation is ongoing, not a one-time act.
N-06§279 stay of suits does NOT apply to Supreme Court / High Court appeals pending at date of winding-up order — Tribunal leave not needed for those.
N-07§334 — any post-commencement disposition of property is void unless Tribunal orders otherwise. Share transfers too. Commencement = date of petition (§357).
N-08CLDUA Account — undistributed assets for 6 months → deposit; unclaimed for 15 years → CG General Revenue. Interest penalty @ 12% p.a. (not 15%).
N-09§278 — Effect of Winding-Up Order: operates in favour of ALL creditors AND contributories as if made on their joint petition — cannot be selective.
N-10§§ 326 & 327 do NOT apply under IBC liquidation — §53 IBC waterfall applies instead. This is a critical IBS crossover point.
IRAC Ready-to-Use Template — §328 Fraudulent Preference (Most Examined Section)
ISSUE
Whether the sale/transfer of company property made by [MD/Director] to [related party] on [date], within six months before the winding-up order dated [date], constitutes a Fraudulent Preference under §328 of the Companies Act, 2013, and whether the Official Liquidator can recover such assets.
RULE
Under §328, Companies Act, 2013, any transfer of property (movable/immovable), delivery of goods, payment, or execution made by or against a company within six months before the winding-up application, which places a creditor / surety / guarantor in a better position than they would have been in a normal liquidation, shall be treated as a Fraudulent Preference. The Tribunal may declare such transaction invalid and restore the pre-transaction position. The SC in IDBI Bank Ltd. v. Official Liquidator affirmed this principle.
APPLICATION
In the present case, the sale occurred on [date] and the winding-up order was passed on [date] — a gap of [X months], which falls within the six-month window. The property was transferred to [related party] in which [MD's son] had an interest, causing a loss of ₹[amount] to the company. This constitutes a clear preferential treatment of an interested party at the expense of general creditors, satisfying the test of fraudulent preference.
CONCLUSION
The transaction is a Fraudulent Preference under §328. The Official Liquidator can apply to the Tribunal to have the transaction declared invalid and the position restored, effectively reversing the transfer and recovering ₹[amount] for the benefit of all creditors.
