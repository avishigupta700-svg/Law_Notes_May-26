Framework One
Source & Contribution Classification Framework
Compliance Indicators — High-Yield for IBS Case Studies
  • NRI (Indian Passport) ≠ Foreign Source. Person of Indian Origin (Foreign Passport) = Foreign Source.
  • Gift in kind from foreign source ≤ ₹1,00,000 for personal use → NOT Foreign Contribution (FC).
  • Interest on FC deposited in FCRA account → IS FC (Explanation 2).
  • Commercial earnings (fee for services) from a foreign client → NOT FC (Explanation 3).
  • UN, World Bank, IMF → Excluded from definition of "Foreign Source."
  • 100% subsidiary of foreign company ≤ FEMA limits → NOT a foreign source.
Decision Tree — Is it "Foreign Contribution"?
IF Transfer/donation/delivery is made by a "Foreign Source"
THEN → Proceed to check nature of transfer
IF Nature = Article (in-kind gift for personal use) AND Market Value ≤ ₹1,00,000
ELSE (NOT FC) → Below threshold gift exemption. No FCRA applicability.
THEN (IS FC) → Article exceeds ₹1,00,000. Registration/Prior Permission needed.
IF Nature = Fee for services / commercial transaction / salary / scholarship
ELSE (NOT FC) → Explanation 3 exclusion. Governed by FEMA, not FCRA.
IF Nature = Interest earned on FC already deposited in FCRA Account
THEN (IS FC) → Explanation 2: income on FC = FC. Must be tracked & reported.
IF Transfer is from an NRI (Indian passport holder)
ELSE (NOT FC) → NRI = Indian citizen. Section 4 exemption. Inform CG if > ₹10L/year from relatives via FC-1.

Framework Two
Prohibition & Restriction Framework
Section 3 — Absolute Prohibitions (Cannot Accept FC)
  • Candidate for election
  • Correspondent/Editor/Publisher of registered newspaper
  • Public Servant, Judge, Government Servant
  • Employee of Govt. corporation/body
  • Member of any Legislature
  • Political party or office-bearer thereof
  • Organisation of political nature (notified u/s 5)
  • Association/company in audio/visual news broadcasting
  • Cartoonist/Editor of such broadcasting association
Section 4 — Exceptions (FC Allowed Even for Prohibited Persons)
  • Salary/wages from foreign source in ordinary business
  • International trade / business outside India
  • As agent of foreign source for Govt. transactions
  • Gift as member of Indian delegation (per CG rules)
  • From a Relative (FC exempt; but if > ₹10L/yr → inform CG via FC-1 within 3 months)
  • Remittance via official banking channels (FEMA)
  • Scholarship, stipend, or similar payment
Section 6 — Foreign Hospitality: Who Needs Prior CG Permission?

Persons requiring prior CG permission before accepting Foreign Hospitality abroad:

  • Members of Legislature
  • Office-bearers of political parties
  • Judges
  • Government servants
  • Employees of Govt. corporations/bodies
Foreign Hospitality [S.2(1)(i)] Any non-casual offer by a foreign source covering travel costs, boarding, lodging, transport or medical treatment to any foreign country.
Emergency Exception No prior permission needed for emergent (sudden) medical illness during foreign visit. BUT intimation to CG within 1 month required — except if value ≤ ₹1,00,000.

Compliance Indicators — Section 7 & 8 (2020 Amendment Impact)
  • Section 7 (Transfer Ban): Even transferring to another FCRA-registered body is now prohibited. This is a 2020 amendment — a favourite exam trick.
  • Section 8 (Admin Cap): Reduced from 50% to 20%. Speculative investments (mutual funds, shares) = absolutely prohibited from FC funds.
  • Debt-based secure investment is NOT treated as speculative — a key distinction for IBS case facts.
Cross-Subject Links — FW-02
FR Admin expense % calculation → impacts NPO accounting policy disclosures. Unspent admin expenses can be carried forward (Form FC-4).
Audit SA 250: Auditor must verify transfer compliance. Section 7 violation = material irregularity. FC-4 must be certified by CA.
DT If FC utilised for speculative purposes (mutual funds) → income may lose Section 11 exemption and become taxable as "income applied not for charitable purposes."

Framework Three
Registration & Permission Framework
Mode I — Certificate of Registration [S.11(1)]
  • Entity must have a definite cultural/economic/educational/religious/social programme
  • Must have been in existence for 3 years
  • Must have audited financials for last 3 years
  • Must have spent at least ₹15 lakhs (excl. admin) for stated objectives
  • Apply in Form FC-3A (electronic) with Proforma AA affidavit
  • Certificate valid for 5 years
  • Renewal: Apply within 6 months before expiry (Form FC-3C); Fee ₹5,000
Mode II — Prior Permission [S.11(2)]
  • For new/formative-stage organisations not yet eligible for registration
  • Specific amount + specific donor + specific project
  • Apply in Form FC-3B (electronic) with Proforma AA affidavit
  • Valid only for the specific purpose / specific amount approved
  • If FC value > ₹1 crore → CG may allow receipt in instalments
  • 2nd instalment released only after 75% utilisation proof + field inquiry
  • No second application within 6 months of first application for same project
Decision Tree — Which Entities Can Apply?
IF Entity = Individual or HUF
THEN → Eligible for Prior Permission. Individual must have no criminal conviction or pending prosecution.
IF Entity = Private Limited Company registered as Section 8 (Companies Act 2013)
THEN → Eligible for Registration or Prior Permission.
IF Entity = Partnership Firm
ELSE (NOT ELIGIBLE) → Partnership Firm cannot obtain registration or prior permission under FCRA.
IF Entity = Govt. body whose accounts are compulsorily audited by C&AG
ELSE (EXEMPT) → Such bodies are exempted from all FCRA provisions.
IF Person's Certificate is currently SUSPENDED
ELSE (NOT ELIGIBLE) → Cannot apply for new certificate or prior permission while suspension continues.
1
Apply in FC-3A / FC-3B electronically. Must have FCRA Account (SBI NDMB) opened and mentioned in application. Provide Aadhaar of all office bearers / directors (residents). Foreigners → copy of Passport or OCI Card [S.12A].
2
CG conducts inquiry, verifies Section 12(4) conditions: entity not fictitious/benami, no communal conviction, no fund misuse, no sedition, no FCRA contravention history, acceptance will not affect sovereignty/integrity/public interest.

CG to grant certificate / prior permission ordinarily within 90 days. If not granted within 90 days → reasons must be communicated to applicant.
4
On rejection → CG records reasons in its order and furnishes copy to applicant. Exception: reasons need not be communicated if information is exempt under RTI Act, 2005.
5
Certificate valid for 5 years. If FC value at time of prior permission > ₹1 crore → instalment-based receipt allowed. 2nd instalment only after 75% utilisation proof + field inquiry.
Cross-Subject Links — FW-03
FR Section 8 company classification in financial statements. Registration status affects consolidation (subsidiary vs. associate) if foreign parent is involved.
Audit SA 265: Auditor must report non-compliance with registration to those charged with governance. Non-FCRA registered entity receiving FC = significant risk area.
DT Section 12A Aadhaar requirement — Aadhaar linkage also required for various Direct Tax compliances. Cross-verification possible by authorities.

Framework Four
Account & Reporting Framework
Section 17 — The Three-Tier FCRA Banking Structure (2020 Amendment)
TIER 1 — Receipt Account Mandatory FCRA Account at SBI, New Delhi Main Branch (NDMB). ALL foreign contribution must first be received here. No local/domestic funds allowed in this account.
TIER 2 — Holding Account Another "FCRA Account" may be opened at any scheduled bank of choice to keep/utilise FC transferred from Tier 1 SBI account.
TIER 3 — Utilisation Accounts One or more accounts at any scheduled bank(s) for actual utilisation of FC transferred from Tier 1/2. Intimation in Form FC-6D to MHA within 15 days of opening.

⚡ Critical: No funds other than FC shall be received/deposited in any FCRA account. Mixing = FCRA violation.

Decision Tree — What Happens After Cancellation?
IF Certificate is cancelled u/s 14
THEN → FC + assets vest with banking authority (SBI NDMB) till CG gives further directions [S.15]
IF 3 years cooling period has NOT elapsed since cancellation
ELSE (CANNOT APPLY) → Fresh registration / prior permission barred. Must wait.
THEN (CAN APPLY) → Apply freshly as per Rule 9 procedures for new registration.
IF Entity subsequently gets re-registered
THEN → Vested FC and assets SHALL be returned by the authority [S.15(3)].
Compliance Indicators — Lifecycle Framework
  • 3-Year Cooling Period: After cancellation, no registration or prior permission possible. A 2.5-year gap is insufficient (Toastea Ltd. illustration-type case).
  • Suspension ≠ Cancellation: During suspension, 25% of unspent FC usable with CG permission; 75% frozen until revocation.
  • Renewal Fee: ₹5,000. No fee + no application = certificate deemed lapsed. Late applications (with grounds) accepted up to 1 year post-expiry with ₹5,000 additional late fee.
  • Section 14(2): No cancellation without giving reasonable opportunity of being heard (natural justice).
Cross-Subject Links — FW-05
FR On cancellation: FC balance vests with banking authority → contingent liability note in financial statements. Assets created from FC may need write-off or transfer disclosure.
Audit Going concern assessment (SA 570) if entity's certificate is suspended/cancelled. Auditor must disclose regulatory non-compliance in audit report.
DT Assets vested with banking authority → Section 45 capital gains implications. Deemed transfer? IBS integration point: income recognition on vesting of assets.

Adjudication & Confiscation — Sections 28, 29, 30

Who can adjudge confiscation?

  • Court of Session — Without limit (within local jurisdiction of seizure)
  • Officer ≥ Asst. Sessions Judge — Only if value ≤ ₹10,00,000

Reasonable opportunity of representation must be given before confiscation order [S.30].

Section 32 — Revision by Central Government
  • CG may revise suo motu OR on application by registered person
  • Suo motu: Only if order is not more than 1 year old
  • Application: Must be filed within 1 year from communication of order (whichever earlier)
  • CG may condone delay for sufficient cause
Section 41 — Compounding Non-imprisonment offences may be compounded before prosecution. Compounding NOT available within 3 years of a previous compounding for similar offence. After 3 years → treated as first offence again. Fee: ₹3,000 (via FCRA Online portal).
Compliance Indicators — Penalty Framework
  • Section 40 Bar: No court can take cognizance of FCRA offences without prior sanction of CG or authorised officer.
  • Section 35 carries highest penalty (5 years) — covers both accepting and assisting in accepting FC in violation.
  • Company officers' liability [S.39]: Directors, managers, secretaries liable along with company unless they prove ignorance + due diligence.
  • Compounding not available if offence is punishable with imprisonment only. IBS case: Check nature of offence before advising compounding.
Cross-Subject Links — FW-06
FR Penalty provision triggers contingent liability disclosure per AS 29 / Ind AS 37. Confiscated assets → derecognise from books and disclose as exceptional item.
Audit SA 250: Auditor must consider FCRA violations. Qualified opinion or emphasis of matter may be warranted if material violation discovered during audit.
DT Penalties paid under S.34/35 → NOT deductible u/s 37 of IT Act (penalty/fine = not business expenditure). Company-level prosecution: potential impact on MAT computation.

IBS Exam Tool
IRAC Templates — Ready-to-Use Answer Frameworks
IRAC Template 1 — Transfer of Foreign Contribution [Section 7]
ISSUE
Whether an FCRA-registered association (X) can transfer its received foreign contribution to another FCRA-registered organisation (Y)?
RULE
Section 7 of FCRA, 2010 as amended by the Foreign Contribution (Regulation) Amendment Act, 2020, imposes a blanket prohibition on transfer of foreign contribution by any registered person to any other person, irrespective of the recipient's registration status.
APPLICATION
Since the 2020 amendment, even transferring to another registered entity is prohibited. Prior to 2020, CG approval-based transfers were permissible. On the given facts, X intends to transfer FC to Y — this clearly violates Section 7 regardless of Y's FCRA status.
CONCLUSION
X cannot transfer the foreign contribution to Y. Doing so would constitute a contravention of Section 7, exposing X to penalty under Section 35 (imprisonment up to 5 years) and potential cancellation of registration under Section 14.
IRAC Template 2 — Speculative Investment of FC [Section 8]
ISSUE
Whether XYZ Foundation (FCRA registered) can invest maturity proceeds from FC bank deposits into Mutual Funds?
RULE
Section 8(1)(a) of FCRA, 2010 prohibits utilisation of FC for speculative business. Rule 4(1) of FCR Rules 2011 defines speculative business to include investment in mutual funds or shares (instruments with risk of appreciation/depreciation). Further, Explanation 2 to S.2(1)(h) deems interest on FC deposits as FC itself.
APPLICATION
The interest earned on FC bank deposits becomes part of FC corpus. Investing this amount in mutual funds = using FC for speculative purposes. The fact that mutual funds are regulated or "safe" is irrelevant — the element of market-linked risk suffices to classify it as speculative under Rule 4.
CONCLUSION
XYZ Foundation cannot invest FC or interest on FC in mutual funds. Debt-based secure instruments would not attract this bar. This restriction applies to the FC corpus as well as all income arising from it.
IRAC Template 3 — Cancellation Cooling Period [Section 14(3)]
ISSUE
Can Toastea Ltd., whose FCRA registration was cancelled 2.5 years ago in public interest, now apply for fresh registration or prior permission?
RULE
Section 14(3) of FCRA, 2010 mandates a mandatory cooling period of 3 years from the date of cancellation of certificate before the entity can be eligible for fresh registration or prior permission. No exceptions.
APPLICATION
Only 2.5 years have elapsed since cancellation. The statutory 3-year cooling period has not been completed. A written declaration of future compliance is not a recognised ground for waiver of this period under FCRA.
CONCLUSION
Toastea Ltd. is not yet eligible. It must wait until the 3-year cooling period (from the cancellation date) is fully completed before applying for fresh registration under Rule 9 of FCR Rules, 2011.
