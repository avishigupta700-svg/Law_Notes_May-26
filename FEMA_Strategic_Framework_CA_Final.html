Framework 01
01
The Residential Status
Classification Framework

The first gate of FEMA analysis — every transaction begins by determining if the entity is PRII (Person Resident in India) or PROI (Person Resident Outside India). Unlike Income Tax, status is date-specific, not year-based.

Decision Tree — Individual Residential Status [Sec. 2(v)]
IF person resided in India > 182 days in preceding FY
THEN → Prima facie PRII
BUT IF left India for: (i) Employment abroad, or (ii) Business abroad, or (iii) Any purpose indicating uncertain stay outside
THEN → Becomes PROI from date of departure
AND IF came to India for: (i) Employment in India, or (ii) Business in India, or (iii) Uncertain stay in India
THEN → PRII from date of arrival
IF resided in India ≤ 182 days in preceding FY
ELSE → PROI (unless came for employment/business/uncertain stay)
NOTE: Company/Body Corporate incorporated in India → always PRII. Branch outside India owned/controlled by PRII → also PRII.
ContextTrigger PointLegal RequirementStrategic Impact
Individual leaves India for higher studies abroad Date of departure PROI from departure date (RBI AP Circular 45/2003 — students = PROI) NRE account must be re-designated; FEMA restrictions on capital account apply
NRI returns to India for uncertain period Date of return with intent to stay PRII from date of return [Basant Kumar Sharma — HC Delhi] NRE account re-designation mandatory; LRS limits kick in
Foreign company opens branch in India Branch controlled by PROI Branch = PRII [Sec. 2(v)(iii)] Indian residents can deal freely; no cross-border restrictions for intra-India dealings
Person on visa (tourist/medical) Purpose of stay is specific / certain period PROI — unless purpose = employment/business/uncertain [K. Ramullan case] Cannot hold Indian rupee accounts as resident; remittances governed by PROI rules
🔗 Cross-Subject Links — IBS Integration
FR (Financial Reporting)
Residential status determines IND AS 21 applicability — functional currency determination and translation of foreign operations. NRI's RFC account balances affect forex disclosure under IND AS 107.
Audit
Auditor must verify client's FEMA residential status when auditing NRE/FCNR accounts, overseas transactions. CARO 2020 — reporting on utilisation of foreign funds [Clause 19].
DT (Direct Tax)
FEMA's 182-day rule ≠ Income Tax 182-day rule. FEMA focuses on purpose; IT focuses on days count. An NRI under IT may be PRII under FEMA — critical difference in IBS case studies.
⚠ Compliance Indicators — Non-Negotiables for Case Study
  • Citizenship is irrelevant — FEMA residential status is based on purpose of stay, not passport.
  • Date-specific status — Unlike IT (year-based), FEMA status changes from the specific date of departure/arrival.
  • Branch owned/controlled by PRII = PRII — Even if physically located abroad.
  • Students = PROI per RBI AP Circular 45/2003, regardless of 182-day count.
  • Green Card holder going abroad = PROI from date of departure (uncertain stay intent).

The Current Account Transaction
Permission Framework [Sec. 5]

Current account transactions are presumptively free — permitted unless specifically restricted. The Central Government may impose reasonable restrictions via the FEM (Current Account Transactions) Rules, 2000.

Golden Rule: CAT Freely permissible unless appearing in Schedule I (Prohibited), Schedule II (CG Approval) or Schedule III (RBI Approval). If not in any schedule → freely permitted.
Decision Tree — Current Account Transaction [CAT Rules 2000]
STEP 1: Is the transaction in Schedule I?
YES → PROHIBITED — Cannot proceed at all (e.g., lottery winnings, callback services)
NO → Go to Step 2
STEP 2: Is it in Schedule II?
YES → CG APPROVAL — Obtain approval from relevant Ministry (e.g., Cultural Tours → Ministry of HRD)
NO → Go to Step 3
STEP 3: Is it in Schedule III?
YES → RBI APPROVAL — Permitted within USD 250,000 per FY under LRS; beyond that → RBI prior approval
NO → FREELY PERMITTED — Transact via Authorised Person
ContextTrigger PointLegal RequirementStrategic Impact
SCH I — PROHIBITED
Lottery winnings, racing, callback services, commission on exports via equity JV/WOS, dividend balancing companies		 Any such remittance attempt Absolutely prohibited — no RBI/CG permission can cure Contravention u/s 13 — penalty up to 3× amount involved
SCH II — CG APPROVAL
Cultural tours, advertisement in foreign print media > USD 10,000, freight of PSU chartered vessels, transponder hiring, prize money > USD 1,00,000 for sports		 Before remittance Prior approval from relevant Ministry (HRD, Finance, Surface Transport, I&B etc.) No exemption even from RFC/EEFC accounts (except specific items)
SCH III — RBI APPROVAL (LRS)
Private visits, gift, emigration, maintenance of relatives, medical, studies abroad		 Above USD 2,50,000 per FY per individual Within USD 2,50,000 → free. Beyond → RBI prior approval (except medical/education with institution estimate) LRS is subsumed in overall USD 2,50,000 limit; minors' forms must be countersigned by guardian
Commission to overseas agent for sale of Indian property Agent's commission per transaction Exceeds USD 25,000 or 5% of inward remittance (whichever more) → RBI approval. Even from EEFC account. Frequently tested in case studies — exact threshold is USD 25,000 or 5%
Consultancy services from abroad Project value Infrastructure projects: USD 10 mn limit without approval; Other: USD 1 mn limit Exceeding limits = RBI approval required
LRS Clubbing Rule: Remittances can be consolidated for family members, EXCEPT capital account transactions (bank accounts, investments, property) — clubbing not permitted unless co-owner/co-partner.
🔗 Cross-Subject Links — IBS Integration
FR
Remittances under LRS → affect Cash Flow Statement (financing/investing activities). Gift to NRI = revenue expenditure for donor? Ind AS 21 — transaction-date rates for CAT.
Audit
Verify Schedule compliance in audit of exporters/importers. SA 250 — auditor's responsibility to detect non-compliance with laws. Hawala transactions = red flag in bank reconciliation audit.
IDT (GST)
Remittances abroad for services = Export of Services (zero-rated) if consideration received in convertible forex. GST refund eligibility depends on FEMA-compliant receipt of payment.
⚠ Compliance Indicators — Non-Negotiables
  • LRS ceiling = USD 2,50,000 per individual per FY — includes both current and capital account.
  • Lottery winnings remittance = Absolutely prohibited — no exceptions under any schedule.
  • Sports prize money > USD 1,00,000 → Ministry of HRD (Youth Affairs) approval needed.
  • RFC/EEFC account exemptions exist for Sch II & III — but NOT for P&I Club and property agent commissions.
  • Payment for import of goods to PROI visiting India in INR — permitted (boarding/lodging expenses).

The Capital Account Transaction
Regulatory Framework [Sec. 6]

Capital account transactions are presumptively prohibited — permitted only if specifically allowed. They alter the asset/liability structure across borders.

Core Test: Does the transaction alter assets or liabilities (including contingent liabilities) outside India for PRII, or alter assets/liabilities in India for PROI? If YES → Capital Account Transaction.
Post Oct 15, 2019 — Dual Regulatory Control:
RBI regulates → Debt Instruments (bonds, loans, debentures, bank borrowings)
CG regulates → Non-Debt Instruments (equity, FDI, immovable property, mutual funds >50% equity, AIFs, REITs)
ContextTrigger PointLegal RequirementStrategic Impact
PRII investing in foreign securities (equities) Any outward investment Permitted under Schedule I — max USD 2,50,000 p.a. under LRS for individuals; 400% of net worth for Indian entities (ODI) Non-debt instrument → governed by CG notification; FDI policy applies
PROI investing in India — Prohibited sectors Any inflow into prohibited activity Prohibited: Chit fund, Nidhi, Agriculture/Plantation, Real estate (pure), TDR trading REITs and township construction allowed; "real estate business" ≠ development
PRII inheriting foreign assets Succession / Inheritance from PROI May hold, own, transfer or invest freely [Sec. 6(4)] — no RBI approval for inherited assets Income from such assets also permitted to be used for fresh investments abroad
Transactions not facing restriction Loan amortisation; Direct investment depreciation No restrictions on drawal for these — Sec. 6(2) proviso Loan repayments are always freely allowed regardless of CAT status
Transactions with North Korea (DPRK) Any capital account transaction Prohibited unless specific CG approval [S.O. 1549(E) 2017]. Existing investments to be wound up within 180 days. Compliance risk — existing investments must be divested promptly
🔗 Cross-Subject Links — IBS Integration
FR
FDI inflows = share capital (equity) or borrowings (debt). Distinction affects IND AS 32 (Financial Instruments: Presentation). ECB with conversion rights → compound financial instrument. FCCB valuation at FVTPL under Ind AS 109.
Audit
CARO 2020 Clause 14 — details of FEMA compliance for capital account transactions to be reported. Auditor checks ODI approvals, ECB LRN, Form FC-GPR filing. Whistleblower if PROI investing in prohibited sector.
DT
Capital account transactions (ODI) → gains on disposal of foreign securities taxed under Sec. 112A / 115F (NRI). Interest on ECB → Sec. 194LC concessional TDS at 5%. DTAA relief for double taxation of capital gains.
⚠ Compliance Indicators — Non-Negotiables
  • Capital Account ≠ freely permitted — opposite presumption to current account.
  • Resident individual USD 2,50,000 limit under LRS applies to both current and capital account — single combined ceiling.
  • Real estate vs. real estate business — Townships, roads, bridges = permitted. Pure buy-sell of real estate = prohibited for PROI.
  • Max 2-layer subsidiary structure for ODI — banking, NBFC (systemically important), insurance, Govt companies are exempted.
  • ODI in start-ups abroad → only from internal accruals (no borrowings).

The External Commercial Borrowing
(ECB) Compliance Framework

ECBs are commercial loans from recognised non-resident lenders to eligible Indian entities. Compliance is multi-parameter — ALL parameters must be satisfied simultaneously.

ParameterFCY ECBINR ECBStrategic Impact
Currency Any freely convertible foreign currency Indian Rupee (INR) FCY ECB → currency risk; INR ECB → lender bears forex risk
Eligible Borrowers All FDI-eligible entities + Port Trusts, SEZ units, SIDBI, EXIM Bank Same + Registered micro-finance entities, NGOs, societies Start-ups: separate framework (USD 3 mn limit p.a.)
Min Avg Maturity (MAMP) Generally 3 years. Manufacturing co. ≤ USD 50 mn → 1 year. Working capital from equity holder → 5 yrs. General corporate (non-equity holder) → 10 yrs Call/put options not exercisable before MAMP
All-in-Cost Ceiling Benchmark + 500 bps Benchmark + 450 bps Includes interest, fees, charges, guarantee fees. Excludes commitment fee, prepayment fee, withholding tax
Automatic Route Limit USD 750 mn per FY Beyond → Approval Route via RBI through AD Cat-I bank
ECB Liability-Equity Ratio 7:1 (from direct foreign equity holder). Not applicable if total ECB ≤ USD 5 mn Exceeding ratio → prior RBI approval required
Negative List (End-Uses Prohibited): Real estate, capital market investment, equity investment, working capital (except for specific categories), general corporate purposes (except specific), repayment of Rupee loans (except specific). These are the most exam-tested prohibitions.
ECB Reporting Compliance Flow
STEP 1: Obtain LRN (Loan Registration Number) from RBI via AD Cat-I bank before any drawdown
STEP 2: Monthly Form ECB-2 Return → within 7 working days of month-end
STEP 3: Changes to ECB terms → revised Form ECB within 7 days of change
STEP 4: Late submissions → Late Submission Fee (LSF) can regularise delay
UNTRACEABLE ENTITY: ECB borrower not reachable for 2+ quarters with 6+ documented attempts → written-off from external debt; Enforcement Directorate notified; no fresh ECB
🔗 Cross-Subject Links — IBS Integration
FR
ECB = financial liability under Ind AS 109 at amortised cost using EIR method. FCCB = compound financial instrument → split into debt and equity components at inception. Hedge accounting under Ind AS 109 for ECB hedges.
Audit
Verify LRN, ECB-2 filings, MAMP compliance, hedging requirements. Infrastructure companies → mandatory 70% hedging for ECB with MAMP <5 yrs — auditor to verify Board-approved risk management policy.
DT
Interest on ECB → TDS under Sec. 194LC at 5% (vs. 20% normal). FCCB conversion to equity → tax neutrality at conversion stage. ECB proceeds for capital expenditure → depreciation benefit.
⚠ Compliance Indicators — Non-Negotiables
  • No drawdown before LRN — this is the most common compliance failure point.
  • ECB for working capital → only permitted for certain borrower categories (equity holders, manufacturing etc.)
  • Indian banks cannot issue guarantees for ECB obligations.
  • Infrastructure companies: 70% mandatory hedging if MAMP < 5 years — Board-approved risk policy required.
  • Conversion of ECB to equity → allowed but requires FDI policy compliance, no additional cost, lender's consent, FC-GPR filing.

The Overseas Investment (OI)
Framework [OI Rules 2022]

The 2022 OI framework replaced the erstwhile ODI/OPI regulations, consolidating them into OI Rules (CG) and OI Regulations (RBI). Goal: simplification and "Ease of Doing Business."

ODI vs OPI Classification [Key Threshold: 10%]
IF investment ≥ 10% of paid-up equity of a listed foreign entity → ODI
IF investment < 10% of paid-up equity of listed entity with control → ODI
IF investment < 10% of paid-up equity without control → OPI
IF investment in any unlisted equity → ODI (regardless of %)
Once classified as ODI → continues as ODI even if shareholding falls below 10% or control is lost [Reg. 2(1)(q) explanation]
ContextTrigger PointLegal RequirementStrategic Impact
ODI by Indian entity — Financial commitment limit Aggregate of all ODI + debt + non-fund commitments 400% of net worth (last audited B/S). Maharatna/Navratna in strategic sectors → no limit. Net worth = audited balance sheet; retained earnings from foreign entity excluded from calculation
OPI by Indian entity All OPI combined Not exceeding 50% of net worth Listed Indian companies may reinvest; unlisted → only specific modes (rights, bonus, ESOPs etc.)
No-objection certificate requirement Before any financial commitment Required if: (i) NPA account, (ii) Wilful defaulter classification, (iii) Under CBI/ED/SFIO investigation Bank/regulator must respond within 60 days → else deemed NOC
Minimum ODI holding period before disinvestment Transfer of ODI Must hold for at least 1 year from ODI date before disinvestment Exception: merger, demerger, amalgamation between wholly-owned entities
Restructuring of loss-making foreign entity Entity with losses for past 2 years (audited) Diminution > USD 10 mn or >20% of outstanding dues → registered valuer certificate required (≤6 months old) Valuer certification date matters — staleness of certificate = non-compliance
ODI prohibition — restricted activities Investment in any of these Prohibited: Real estate, Gambling, Financial products linked to INR (without RBI approval) Townships/roads/bridges NOT considered "real estate" — permitted
🔗 Cross-Subject Links — IBS Integration
FR
ODI in subsidiary → consolidation under Ind AS 110. ODI in associate → equity method (Ind AS 28). OPI → FVTPL/FVOCI under Ind AS 109. Annual Performance Report (APR) → feeds into IND AS 107 disclosures.
Audit
Annual Performance Report (APR) by Dec 31 each year — based on audited financials of foreign entity. Annual Return on FLA to RBI. Auditor checks UIN, AD bank designation, evidence of investment (share certs within 6 months).
DT
Dividends from foreign subsidiaries → taxed in India as "income from other sources." Sec. 80C/115A not applicable for ODI income. Transfer pricing implications for related-party transactions with ODI entities.
⚠ Compliance Indicators — Non-Negotiables
  • UIN before remittance — Unique Identification Number must be obtained from RBI before any outward remittance for ODI.
  • Evidence of investment within 6 months — share certificates or equivalent documents to AD bank.
  • No further commitment if reporting delayed — restriction applies until delay is regularised.
  • ODI in start-ups abroad: only from internal accruals — individual's own funds only, no borrowed funds.
  • Pakistan and CG-notified jurisdictions → prior CG approval mandatory for all OI.

The Export Realisation &
Repatriation Framework [Sec. 7 & 8]

Every exporter is obligated to realise and repatriate export proceeds — foreign exchange belongs to the nation, not merely the individual exporter.

ContextTrigger PointLegal RequirementStrategic Impact
Normal export of goods/software/services Date of export / invoice Realise and repatriate within 9 months Non-realisation = contravention u/s 13
Export to overseas warehouse Date of shipment Realise within 15 months RBI permission required to establish overseas warehouse
Export documents submission Date of export / SOFTEX certification Submit to AD bank within 21 days AD bank may accept late submission for reasons beyond exporter's control
Advance received from buyer Date of receipt of advance payment Shipment within 1 year. Interest: max Benchmark + 100 bps. Refund if unable: before 1 year (else RBI approval) Exception: export agreement provides for shipment beyond 1 year
Import payment settlement Date of shipment Pay within 6 months. AD can extend up to 6 months at a time (max 3 years for disputes) Deferred payment for capital goods: up to 3 years; non-capital: up to 1 year or operating cycle
Gift exports exemption Gift of goods No declaration required if value ≤ ₹5 lakhs Trade samples, personal effects, ship stores also exempt
🔗 Cross-Subject Links — IBS Integration
FR
Export proceeds receivable = financial asset (Ind AS 109). Impairment of export receivables if realisation beyond 9 months is doubtful. EEFC account balances → disclosed as cash equivalents or forex assets per Ind AS 7.
Audit
CARO 2020 Clause 18 — report on default in repayment of dues. Export realisation non-compliance must be flagged. SA 250 — FEMA violations = qualified or emphasis-of-matter paragraph.
IDT / GST
Export of goods/services = Zero-rated under GST (Sec. 16, IGST Act). Advance payment → time of supply = date of advance or invoice, whichever earlier. Non-realisation in forex → refund of ITC may be reversed.
⚠ Compliance Indicators — Non-Negotiables
  • 9-month rule is critical — failure to realise within 9 months = contravention, even if RBI hasn't given specific direction.
  • EDF form for physical exports; SOFTEX for software — specific forms, specific authorities.
  • Import settlement: 6 months from date of shipment (not from date of invoice).
  • Extension of import dues beyond 1 year: outstanding must not exceed USD 1 mn or 10% of average import remittances (2 FYs), whichever lower.
  • Omission of RBI to give directions does not absolve the exporter of compliance obligations.

The Authorised Person (AP)
Regulatory Framework [Sec. 10-12]

All forex dealings must route through Authorised Persons — the RBI-designated intermediary gatekeepers of FEMA compliance.

Types of Authorised Persons: (i) Authorised Dealer (AD Cat-I, Cat-II), (ii) Money Changer, (iii) Off-Shore Banking Unit (OBU — in SEZ), (iv) Any other RBI-authorised person.
OBU Restriction: An Off-Shore Banking Unit (branch of Indian bank in SEZ) — cannot deal with residents in India. May deal with SEZ units and with other ADs on principal-to-principal basis only.
ContextTrigger PointLegal RequirementPenalty
AP contravenes RBI direction or fails to file return Any direction non-compliance Hear before penalty imposition (reasonable opportunity) Up to ₹10,000 + ₹2,000/day for continuing contravention [Sec. 11]
AP must refuse transaction Person refuses to furnish declaration or info Refuse in writing. If contravention suspected → report to RBI AP's liability if it facilitates a non-compliant transaction
Revocation of authorisation Non-compliance / public interest Must give reasonable opportunity of representation before revocation (except public interest ground) Loss of licence to deal in forex
RBI inspection of AP Any time RBI deems necessary AP must produce books and furnish statements. Directors/partners have equal duty. Obstruction of inspection = contravention
⚠ Compliance Indicators — Non-Negotiables
  • Every forex transaction must route through AP — direct dealings between PRII and PROI in foreign currency = prohibited (Section 3).
  • Person acquires forex and misuses it (different purpose / doesn't surrender) = deemed contravention.
  • OBU is NOT an Authorised Dealer under FEMA — distinct category.

The Enforcement, Penalties &
Appellate Framework [Sec. 13–19, 35–37]

FEMA is a civil law (not criminal, unlike erstwhile FERA). Penalties are monetary; imprisonment is for non-payment of penalty, not for the contravention itself (except Sec. 13(1C) cases).

SectionContraventionQuantum of PenaltyStrategic Impact
Sec. 11 AP contravenes RBI direction / fails to file return ≤ ₹10,000 + ₹2,000/day continuing Reasonable opportunity before penalty imposition
Sec. 13(1) Any contravention of FEMA provisions, rules, regulations Quantifiable: ≤ 3× amount involved
Not quantifiable: ≤ ₹2 lakhs
Continuing: + ₹5,000/day		 Adjudicating Authority may also order confiscation of property/forex
Sec. 13(1A) Acquisition of foreign exchange/security/immovable property outside India exceeding threshold under Sec. 37A ≤ 3× amount involved + confiscation of equivalent Indian assets PMLA/FEMA overlap — joint Enforcement Directorate action
Sec. 13(1C) Repeat contraventions related to Sec. 13(1A) 13(1A) penalties + imprisonment up to 5 years + fine Only criminal element in FEMA — rare but high impact
Sec. 14 Non-payment of penalty within 90 days Civil imprisonment: ≤ 3 years (demand > ₹1 crore); ≤ 6 months (others) Release = immediate on payment. Arrears survive release.

Compounding [Sec. 15]: Voluntary settlement of contravention. Once compounded → no further proceedings. [Sterlite Industries case]. Compounding closes the matter permanently.
Corporate Liability [Sec. 42]: Company commits contravention → Every person "in charge of and responsible for conduct of business" = guilty, UNLESS proves: (i) no knowledge, or (ii) due diligence exercised. Directors, managers, company secretaries with consent/connivance → personally liable.
Death / Insolvency [Sec. 43]: Proceedings do not abate. Obligations devolve on legal representative / official receiver. Legal representative's liability = limited to extent of estate inherited.
🔗 Cross-Subject Links — IBS Integration
FR
Penalty provisions → contingent liabilities disclosure under Ind AS 37. Ongoing FEMA proceedings = "possible obligation" → disclose but don't provide. Compounded amounts = definite liability → provide in financials.
Audit
SA 250 — if FEMA contravention discovered, auditor must assess materiality. Compounding history = KYC risk indicator. Director liability under Sec. 42 → going concern assessment may be affected.
DT / IDT
Penalty paid under FEMA → not deductible u/s 37(1) (expenditure of illegal nature). Enforcement Directorate and PMLA overlap → asset attachment under PMLA can precede FEMA adjudication.
⚠ Compliance Indicators — Non-Negotiables
  • 90 days to pay penalty — non-payment = civil imprisonment (not criminal).
  • FEMA = Civil law — burden of proof: "balance of probabilities" not "beyond reasonable doubt."
  • Appeal to High Court only on question of law — not on facts.
  • Compounding application = 180 days to process — contravener must apply proactively.
  • Section 42 personal liability of directors — "due diligence" defence is available.
  • BCCI case — Associations/Societies ≠ "company" under Sec. 42 explanation — cannot be penalised as company.

IRAC Answer Template — Most
High-Yield Section: LRS & Schedule III

The Liberalised Remittance Scheme (LRS) under Schedule III appears in virtually every IBS case study involving NRI/resident transactions. Use this template as a structural guide.

IRAC Framework — LRS / Schedule III Remittance Problem
ISSUE
Whether [Name], a Person Resident in India (PRII), is permitted to remit USD [X] for [purpose: studies/medical/gift/travel] without prior approval of the Reserve Bank of India under the Foreign Exchange Management Act, 1999, and if so, to what extent.
RULE
Under Section 5 of FEMA read with Schedule III of the FEM (Current Account Transactions) Rules, 2000, an individual PRII may remit up to USD 2,50,000 per financial year (April–March) for permissible current account transactions without RBI prior approval. For [studies/medical treatment], the individual may further remit in excess of this limit, without RBI approval, based on an estimate/requirement certificate from the [university/hospital] abroad. Any amount beyond the LRS ceiling for other purposes requires RBI's prior approval.
APPLICATION
In the present case, [Name]'s cumulative remittances in FY [XXXX] amount to USD [Y]. The proposed remittance of USD [X] for [purpose] falls [within / outside] the USD 2,50,000 LRS ceiling. [If applicable: Since purpose is studies/medical, the excess beyond USD 2,50,000 is also permitted based on the institute/hospital estimate.] [If LRS already utilised: Since USD [Y] has already been remitted this FY, only USD [2,50,000 – Y] remains available, and RBI prior approval is required for the balance of USD [Z].]
CONCLUSION
[Name] [is / is not] required to obtain RBI's prior approval. The remittance [is freely permitted within LRS / requires RBI approval for the amount exceeding USD 2,50,000 / is partially permissible up to the LRS ceiling]. All transactions must be routed through an Authorised Person (Authorised Dealer) as required under Section 10 of FEMA, 1999.
IBS Case Study Pro-Tips
  • Always aggregate all remittances in the FY — the question often gives multiple remittances to test whether LRS ceiling is breached.
  • For medical/education: LRS ceiling is a soft limit — can go beyond with institution's estimate certificate.
  • Check if RFC/EEFC account exempts from Schedule II/III requirements — this is a frequent differentiator.
  • Identify if the "person" is an individual or corporate — LRS applies only to individuals; corporates use ODI route.
  • In capital account scenarios, always establish PRII or PROI status first — the entire analysis flows from this.
