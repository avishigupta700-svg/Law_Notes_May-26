Framework 1 · Sections 2(18), 2(19), 2(24), 2(51), 2(53), 2(54), 2(78), 2(94) The Definitional Architecture Framework

Who qualifies as what — foundational to every appointment/remuneration question.

Managing Director [S.2(54)]
  • Must be a director first — loses MD status if ceases to be director
  • Entrusted with substantial powers of management
  • Source of power: Articles / Agreement / Board Resolution / GM Resolution
  • Routine acts excluded: affixing seal, drawing cheques, endorsing negotiable instruments, signing share certificates, transfer of shares
⚡ IBS Trigger: Cannot appoint MD + Manager simultaneously [S.196(1)]
Manager [S.2(53)]
  • Individual with management of whole or substantially whole affairs
  • Works under superintendence of Board
  • Not applicable to departmental heads (e.g., Marketing Manager, Finance Manager)
  • May or may not be a director
WTD [S.2(94)]
  • Director in whole-time employment
  • Must be director — cannot be WTD without being director
KMP Composition [S.2(51)] — Who Must Be Present
Tier 1: Executive
  • CEO or MD or Manager
  • Whole-Time Director (if no CEO/MD/M)
Tier 2: Governance
  • Company Secretary
  • Chief Financial Officer
Tier 3: Board-Designated
  • Officer not more than one level below directors, in whole-time employment
  • Any other as prescribed
FR: KMP disclosures in Related Party Transactions [IND AS 24] Audit: KMP remuneration disclosed in CARO 2020 DT: Perquisites of MD/WTD taxable u/s 17(2) Income Tax Act

Framework 2 · Section 196 · Schedule V Part I The Appointment Compliance Framework

Decision Tree: Appointment of MD / WTD / Manager
🔍 IF Company proposes to appoint MD / WTD / Manager
↓ Step 1: Eligibility Check [S.196(3)]
✅ Person must NOT be disqualified u/s 164 (director disqualifications)
✅ Age: between 21 years and 70 years (ordinary route)
✅ Not undischarged insolvent or adjudicated insolvent
✅ Not convicted & sentenced for > 6 months
✅ Resident of India (12 months continuous stay for employment/business) — not applicable for SEZ companies
✅ No conviction under 19 specified Acts (Income Tax, Customs, FEMA, IBC, GST, SEBI, etc.)
↓ Step 2: Age Exception [S.196(3)(a) proviso]
🔍 IF candidate has attained age ≥ 70 years
↓ THEN (Route A)
Pass Special Resolution in GM (with explanatory statement giving justification)
↓ ELSE IF Special Resolution fails but majority voted in favour
Apply to Central Government (Form MR-2) within 90 days — CG satisfied it is beneficial → appointment valid
↓ Step 3: Approval Process [S.196(4)]
Board approves at a Board Meeting
Shareholders approve at Next General Meeting
Notice must include: terms, remuneration, director's interest in appointment
File MR-1 with ROC within 60 days of appointment
↓ Step 4: Variance from Schedule V?
🔍 IF appointment is at variance to Schedule V Part I conditions
↓ THEN
Seek approval of Central Government (Form MR-2 within 90 days)
Tenure Rules — Critical Traps
Maximum Tenure

No appointment / re-appointment for a term exceeding 5 years at a time [S.196(2)(a)]

Earliest Re-appointment

Re-appointment cannot be made earlier than 1 year before expiry of current term [S.196(2)(b)]

⚠ Common exam trap: re-appointment done 2 years early = INVALID

Validity of Acts Before Shareholder Approval [S.196(5)]
  • Even if GM does not approve the appointment, all acts done by MD/WTD/Manager before the GM are deemed valid
  • Protects third-party transactions
  • Exemption Government Companies: S.196(2),(4),(5) do not apply (subject to no default in filing financial statements)
  • Exemption Private Companies: S.196(4),(5) do not apply (subject to no filing default)
FR: Disclosure of MD/WTD remuneration in Notes to Accounts — IND AS 24 RPT Audit: CARO 2020 requires reporting on managerial remuneration paid in excess of limits DT: Remuneration is deductible expense u/s 37(1) — subject to arm's length / Schedule V compliance IBS: Age >70 + No Special Resolution → CG approval required — classic case-study scenario

Framework 3 · Section 203 · Rules 8, 8A The KMP Appointment & Governance Framework

Which Companies Must Have Whole-Time KMPs?
Company Type Trigger Threshold Mandatory KMPs Rule
Listed Company All listed companies MD/CEO/M + CS + CFO Rule 8
Public Company Paid-up capital ≥ ₹10 Cr MD/CEO/M + CS + CFO Rule 8
Private Company Paid-up capital ≥ ₹10 Cr Whole-time Company Secretary only Rule 8A
Decision Tree: Can One Person Be Chairperson + MD/CEO?
🔍 IF Company proposes to appoint same person as Chairperson AND MD/CEO [S.203(1) Proviso]
↓ DEFAULT RULE: PROHIBITED
❌ Individual cannot hold both positions simultaneously
↓ EXCEPTIONS (Any one of):
✅ Exception 1: Articles of Association expressly permit it
✅ Exception 2: Company does not carry multiple businesses
✅ Exception 3: Company is in multiple businesses AND has appointed separate CEO for each business (Public Co. with paid-up ≥ ₹100 Cr + turnover ≥ ₹1000 Cr) [MCA Notification 2014]
Bar on Multiple Appointments [S.203(3)]
General Rule

A whole-time KMP cannot hold office in more than one company at the same time

Exception — Subsidiary

Can hold office in subsidiary company of the same company

Also: Can be director in any company with Board permission (not disentitled)

MD in More Than One Company [Third Proviso to S.203(3)]
Conditions for valid appointment:
  1. Person should be MD/Manager in one company only + of not more than one other company
  2. Board Resolution passed with consent of ALL directors present at the meeting
  3. Specific notice of meeting and the resolution given to all directors then in India
⚡ Exam Trap: If even one director present voted against / abstained → resolution is INVALID [Example 4 in chapter]

Framework 4 · Section 197 · Schedule V Part II The Remuneration Limits Framework

Applies only to Public Companies. Private companies are exempt from S.197.

Section 197(1): When Company HAS PROFITS — Percentage Limits
Scenario Normal Limit (Net Profit) How to Exceed
Overall (all directors including MD+WTD+M) 11% Special Resolution in GM + Schedule V conditions
One MD / WTD / Manager 5% Special Resolution in GM
More than one MD/WTD/M (combined) 10% Special Resolution in GM
Other Directors (if MD/WTD/M exists) 1% Special Resolution in GM
Other Directors (if NO MD/WTD/M) 3% Special Resolution in GM
⚠ Sitting Fees [S.197(5)] are excluded from the above percentages. Max sitting fee = ₹1 Lakh per meeting. Independent Directors and Women Directors cannot be paid less than other directors. [Rule 4]
When Company Has NO / INADEQUATE PROFITS — Schedule V Part II Section II
Effective Capital (Last FY) Max Managerial Remuneration / Year Max for Other Directors / Year
Negative or < ₹5 Cr ₹60 Lakhs ₹12 Lakhs
₹5 Cr – < ₹100 Cr ₹84 Lakhs ₹17 Lakhs
₹100 Cr – < ₹250 Cr ₹120 Lakhs ₹24 Lakhs
≥ ₹250 Cr ₹120 Lakhs + 0.01% of EC above ₹250 Cr ₹24 Lakhs + 0.01% of EC above ₹250 Cr
⚡ Limits above may be exceeded if shareholders pass a Special Resolution. Also: if company has defaulted on bank dues, obtain prior approval of creditor/bank before GM approval.
Effective Capital — Formula
EFFECTIVE CAPITAL = Paid-up Share Capital (excl. share application money) + Share Premium Account + Reserves & Surplus (excl. Revaluation Reserve) + Long-Term Loans & Deposits (repayable after 1 year; excl. working capital loans, OD, bank guarantee) – Investments (excl. investment companies) – Accumulated Losses – Preliminary Expenses not written off
Perquisites NOT Counted in Remuneration Ceiling [Schedule V Sec. IV]
For All Managerial Persons:
  • PF / Superannuation / Annuity (non-taxable portion)
  • Gratuity ≤ ½ month salary per completed year
  • Encashment of leave at end of tenure
Additional for Expatriates (incl. NRIs):
  • Children's education allowance (max ₹12,000/month/child, max 2 children)
  • Holiday passage for family staying abroad (economy class yearly / first class biennial)
  • Leave travel concession (home country)
FR: IND AS 19 — Post-employment benefits (Gratuity/PF) measured at actuarial present value Audit: S.197(16) — Auditor must report in Audit Report whether remuneration is in accordance with S.197 DT: Perquisites of MD valued per Rule 3, Income Tax Rules — includes accommodation, ESOP, etc. IBS: Effective Capital computation is a direct numerical question in case studies

Framework 5 · Section 198 The Net Profit Calculation Framework (for Managerial Remuneration)

NET PROFIT u/s 198 = Book Profit Before Tax ADD: Bounties & Subsidies from Government Capital Repairs (non-deductible repairs added back) Directors' Remuneration (if already charged) LESS: Capital Profits on sale of fixed assets* Premium on issue/sale of shares or debentures Profits on sale of forfeited shares Profits of capital nature (sale of undertaking) Unrealised gains / Notional gains / Revaluation gains Fair value changes in equity reserves NOT DEDUCTED: Income Tax, Capital Losses, Voluntary damages
* Capital Profit Exception: Credit IS given for profit on sale of fixed asset to the extent of: min(Sale Price – WDV, Original Cost – WDV). Only the excess over original cost is excluded.
Deductible Expenses u/s 198(4) [Always charged; already in P&L — no double count]
  • All usual working charges (salaries, wages)
  • Repairs (non-capital)
  • Compensation / damages under legal liability
  • Interest on debentures, mortgages, unsecured loans
  • Depreciation u/s 123
  • Directors' remuneration
  • Bad debts written off
  • Outgoings incl. charitable contributions u/s 181
FR: Profit u/s 198 ≠ Profit as per Ind AS — reconciliation required for remuneration working DT: Depreciation u/s 123 (Companies Act) vs. depreciation u/s 32 (Income Tax) — different bases IBS: Section 198 computation is a high-value numerical — appears regularly in case studies

Framework 6 · Sections 197(9),(10) · Section 199 The Excess Remuneration Recovery Framework

Refund of Excess [S.197(9)]
  • Director draws excess → must refund within 2 years (or lesser period allowed by company)
  • Until refund: holds amount in trust for company
  • Waiver possible only by Special Resolution within 2 years of the sum becoming refundable
  • If default on bank dues → need prior bank/creditor approval before GM waiver resolution
Recovery on Restatement [S.199]
  • Financial statements restated due to fraud or non-compliance
  • Company recovers excess from past or present MD/WTD/Manager/CEO
  • Excess = Actual remuneration received – Remuneration as per restated financials
  • Recovery does not absolve person from other liabilities under the Act
FR: If financial statements restated → prior year adjustments; IND AS 8 retrospective application Audit: Restatement triggers → SA 560 (Subsequent Events) & SA 240 (Fraud) IBS: Recovery from past MD — applicable even after retirement [Option (b) in MCQ 1]

Framework 7 · Section 202 The Loss-of-Office Compensation Framework

🔍 IF MD / WTD / Manager loses office — is compensation payable?
↓ CHECK: Is this a prohibited case?
❌ NO COMPENSATION if director: (1) Resigned voluntarily | (2) Resigned on reconstruction but gets re-appointed in reconstructed entity | (3) Office vacated under S.167(1) | (4) Company wound up due to director's negligence/default | (5) Guilty of fraud/breach of trust/gross negligence/gross mismanagement | (6) Instigated or directly/indirectly caused termination of own office
↓ IF NONE of the above → Compensation IS payable
✅ Quantum: Remuneration for remainder of term OR 3 years, whichever is SHORTER
✅ Calculation Base: Average remuneration earned in last 3 years (or actual period if < 3 years)
❌ NO compensation if winding up commenced before or within 12 months of loss of office AND assets insufficient to repay share capital
📌 Bell v. Lever Bros. [1932]: A director is not legally bound to disclose breaches of fiduciary duty. Company cannot recover ad-hoc compensation paid if it subsequently discovers past misconduct (absent statutory remedy).
FR: Compensation provision to be recognised u/s IND AS 37 — present obligation, probable outflow DT: Compensation for loss of office taxable u/s 17(3) as 'profits in lieu of salary' IBS: Classic exam scenario — termination + fraud allegation → no compensation payable

Framework 8 · Sections 204, 205 The CS Functions & Secretarial Audit Framework

Functions of CS [S.205 + Rule 10]
  • Report to Board on compliance of Companies Act + other laws
  • Ensure compliance with Secretarial Standards (SS-1, SS-2)
  • Provide guidance to directors on duties/responsibilities/powers
  • Convene, attend & maintain minutes of Board/Committee/GM
  • Obtain approvals from Board, GM, Government, Regulators
  • Represent before regulators u/s Companies Act
  • Assist & advise Board on corporate governance
  • Any other duties assigned by Board
⚠ CS functions do not reduce or absolve duties of Board, Chairperson, MD, or WTD [S.205(2)]
Secretarial Audit [S.204 + Rule 9]
Who must get it done?
  • Every listed company
  • Public company: paid-up capital ≥ ₹50 Cr
  • Public company: turnover ≥ ₹250 Cr
  • Any company: outstanding loans/borrowings from banks/PFIs ≥ ₹100 Cr
Who conducts it?
  • Company Secretary in practice only (Form MR-3)
Filing?
  • Annexed to Board's Report [S.134(3)]
  • Board must explain every qualification/observation in Board's Report [S.204(3)]
Penalty for non-compliance:
  • Company / officer / CS in practice: ₹2 Lakhs each
Audit: Secretarial Audit Report is different from Statutory Audit Report — both go with Board's Report IBS: Outstanding borrowings ₹100 Cr threshold — triggers secretarial audit even for private/unlisted firms

High-Yield Points · IBS Case Study Non-Negotiables Critical Compliance Indicators

These are the points most likely to earn marks — always check these in any IBS case study involving companies.

🔴 Non-Negotiable #1 — Dual Appointment Prohibition
A company cannot appoint both MD and Manager simultaneously [S.196(1)]. If case study shows both — flag as violation. WTD + MD is allowed.
🔴 Non-Negotiable #2 — Re-appointment Timing Trap
Re-appointment cannot happen more than 1 year before expiry. If done 2+ years before → invalid. Always compute timeline carefully.
🔴 Non-Negotiable #3 — S.197 Applies Only to Public Companies
Remuneration limits (11%, 5%, etc.) do not apply to private companies. A common case-study distractor.
🔴 Non-Negotiable #4 — Auditor Must Report on Remuneration [S.197(16)]
The auditor's report must state: (a) whether remuneration is in accordance with S.197, (b) whether any director is paid in excess of limits. This is a mandatory audit procedure.
🔴 Non-Negotiable #5 — Effective Capital ≠ Net Worth
Effective Capital excludes revaluation reserve, share application money, working capital loans, bank guarantees. Always compute from first principles in numerical questions.
🔴 Non-Negotiable #6 — Insurance Premium Treatment [S.197(13)]
Premium on indemnity insurance for MD/WTD/Manager = NOT remuneration unless the person is proved guilty — then it becomes remuneration.
🔴 Non-Negotiable #7 — Compensation for Loss of Office: Fraud = No Compensation
If fraud/gross negligence/gross mismanagement is alleged and proven — no compensation payable. But mere allegations are not enough; payment cannot be withheld arbitrarily.

Ready-to-Use · IRAC Answer Template IRAC Framework: Remuneration in Case of Inadequate Profits

Most commonly tested scenario — use this structure for any S.197(3) + Schedule V question.

Issue
Whether a public company that has incurred losses / has inadequate profits can pay remuneration to its Managing Director / WTD / Manager, and if so, what is the maximum permissible amount?
Rule
As per Section 197(3) of the Companies Act, 2013, where a company has no profits or inadequate profits in any financial year, it shall not pay remuneration to its directors (including MD/WTD/Manager) except in accordance with the provisions of Schedule V. Section II of Part II of Schedule V prescribes the maximum remuneration payable based on the effective capital of the company. The limits are: (i) Negative or < ₹5 Cr — ₹60 Lakhs; (ii) ₹5–100 Cr — ₹84 Lakhs; (iii) ₹100–250 Cr — ₹120 Lakhs; (iv) ≥ ₹250 Cr — ₹120 Lakhs + 0.01% of EC above ₹250 Cr. These limits may be exceeded only by a Special Resolution of shareholders. The approval of the Board and Nomination & Remuneration Committee is also required.
Application
In the given case, the company has [losses / inadequate profits]. The effective capital as computed stands at ₹[___], which falls in the bracket of [relevant slab]. Accordingly, the maximum permissible remuneration to the managerial person is ₹[___] per annum. The actual remuneration proposed / paid is ₹[___], which [is within / exceeds] the prescribed limit. [If exceeds: A Special Resolution of shareholders is necessary to validate the payment.]
Conclusion
The payment of remuneration of ₹[___] to [name] as Managing Director is [valid within the Schedule V limit / valid only upon passing a Special Resolution / invalid as it violates Schedule V without Special Resolution].
