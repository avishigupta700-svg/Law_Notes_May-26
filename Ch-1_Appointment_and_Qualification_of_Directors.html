Framework 01

The DIN & Director Qualification Framework

§ Core Definition — Sec 2(34) & Sec 153–159
A Director is a person appointed to the Board of Directors of a company. Every individual intending to be appointed as director must possess a Director Identification Number (DIN) — a unique, lifetime, 8-digit identifier issued by the Central Government.

Key Numeric Anchors — DIN

30
Days to Intimate DIN
Company must inform ROC within 30 days of receiving DIN from director [Sec 157]
₹50K
Min Penalty — DIN Default
Director who acts without DIN: ₹50,000 penalty. Company also liable.
1 Yr
Surrender of DIN
Unused DIN can be surrendered; CG can cancel/deactivate DIN [Sec 155]
ONE
DIN Per Individual
No individual shall have or apply for more than one DIN [Sec 155]

Decision-Tree: DIN Application Process

⬡ If-Then-Else: Obtaining & Using DIN
IF
Individual does NOT have a DIN and intends to become director
→
Apply in Form DIR-3 with CG (MCA Portal) along with supporting documents + fee
1
CG allots DIN → Person intimates company → Company files Form DIR-12 within 30 days to ROC
IF
Person already holds a DIN
→
Use existing DIN; must update KYC annually via Form DIR-3 KYC (by 30 September each year)
✗
DIN not updated/KYC not filed
!
DIN is deactivated → Director cannot be appointed → Penalty of ₹5,000 for late KYC filing

Qualification & Disqualification at a Glance Sec 164

Dimension Qualification Key Disqualifications [Sec 164(1)] Strategic Impact
Legal Capacity Must be an individual; natural person only Unsound mind (court order), undischarged insolvent Body corporate / LLP cannot be director
Age Min age: 18 years; No upper limit in Act (articles may restrict) — Articles may restrict; check AoA before appointment
Criminal Record — Convicted of moral turpitude for ≥6 months (within 5 years of sentence completion); or convicted for any offence and imprisoned ≥7 years Due-diligence check mandatory; disclosures in Prospectus
Company Default [Sec 164(2)] — Director of company that has not filed financial statements / annual returns for 3 continuous years, OR failed to repay deposits/debentures/dividends for ≥1 year 5-Year Bar on re-appointment in any company. Automatic disqualification.
Outstanding Calls — Calls in arrears for 6 months Applicable to public companies; check at time of AGM
Court/Tribunal Order — Order of disqualification passed by Court/NCLT NCLT can remove; check for ongoing NCLT proceedings
⚠ Critical: Sec 164(2) — The "3-Year Filing Default" Trap
Trigger: Company fails to file FS/AR for 3 consecutive financial years OR fails to repay deposits/debentures/interest/dividend declared for ≥1 year.
Consequence: Every director is disqualified for 5 years from being director in any company. ROC publishes the list.
IBS Alert: In case-study, check directors of shell companies or non-compliant companies — they are automatically disqualified even without any court order.

Framework 03

The Director Appointment Process Framework

Types of Directors & Mode of Appointment Sec 152–161

Type Section Appointment Authority Key Trigger / Condition
First Directors Sec 152 Named in Articles of Association; else subscribers to MoA Hold office till first AGM
Director by Shareholders Sec 152 General Meeting — Ordinary Resolution (by rotation) At least 2/3 of directors must be rotational in public companies
Additional Director Sec 161(1) Board of Directors (if AoA so authorises) Holds office till next AGM only; Max tenure: next AGM
Alternate Director Sec 161(2) Board (if AoA authorises + approval of absent director) Original director absent from India for >3 months; cannot hold if original director returns; alternate ceases when original returns
Nominee Director Sec 161(3) Third party (institution/creditor) under agreement, or CG/SG Lender-driven; check loan covenants for nominee right
Casual Vacancy Sec 161(4) Board can fill only if original was appointed by General Meeting Casual appointee holds office for remaining tenure of predecessor only
Director by Proportional Representation Sec 163 If AoA provides — by cumulative voting / other system Minority shareholder protection mechanism

Decision-Tree: Rotational Director Retirement Sec 152(6)

⬡ Retirement by Rotation Logic
IF
Company is a Public Company
→
Minimum 2/3 of total directors must be liable to retire by rotation
2
At every AGM, 1/3 of rotational directors retire (those longest in office retire first)
3
Retiring directors are eligible for re-appointment (unless disqualified)
IF
AGM does not appoint successor AND does not expressly resolve not to fill vacancy
→
Meeting is deemed adjourned to same day next week; if vacancy not filled at that meeting → retiring director deemed re-appointed [Sec 152(7)]
⚠ Consent to Act as Director — Sec 152(5)
Every person appointed as director must file Form DIR-2 (written consent) with company before appointment. Company files DIR-12 with ROC within 30 days of appointment.
Exception: First directors and Government nominees need not file consent.
Framework 04

The Independent Director Compliance Framework

§ Definition — Sec 149(6)
An Independent Director (ID) is a director who is NOT a managing or whole-time director or nominee, AND satisfies the independence criteria specified in Sec 149(6): no material pecuniary relationship, not a promoter, not KMP or relative of KMP, etc.

Applicability: Who Must Have IDs?

Context Trigger Point Requirement Strategic Impact
Listed Company All listed companies At least 1/3 of total board strength SEBI LODR also mandates ≥50% IDs if Chairperson is executive/promoter
Unlisted Public Company Paid-up capital ≥₹10 Cr OR turnover ≥₹100 Cr OR outstanding loans/borrowings ≥₹50 Cr At least 2 Independent Directors Private companies generally exempt; check threshold triggers in case study
Audit Committee Companies required to have Audit Committee Majority must be IDs; Chairperson must be ID FR Link: Auditor independence; Audit Link: SA 260 communication
Nomination & Remuneration Committee Same as above Minimum 3 Non-Executive Directors; majority IDs; Chairperson must be ID KMP remuneration oversight; impact on executive compensation disclosures

Tenure & Re-appointment — Decision Tree Sec 149(10) & (11)

⬡ Independent Director Tenure Logic
1
ID can serve up to 5 consecutive years per term [Sec 149(10)]
IF
Company wishes to re-appoint same ID for a second term
→
Requires Special Resolution + disclosure in Board Report Before expiry of first term
2
Maximum 2 consecutive terms (i.e., 10 years). After that, cooling-off period of 3 years mandatory [Sec 149(11)]
✗
During cooling-off period
!
Cannot be re-appointed as ID in the same company; also cannot be appointed as any other director in the same company during cooling-off
Note
IDs are NOT liable to retire by rotation. They are appointed by shareholders in General Meeting (Ordinary Resolution for first appointment; SR for second term)

Independence Criteria — Key Negative Tests Sec 149(6)

⚠ An ID Must NOT Be / Have:
  • MD, WTD, or Nominee Director of the company
  • Promoter of the company or its group companies
  • Related to any promoter, director, or KMP of the company
  • Had pecuniary relationship >2% of gross turnover / total income with company / holding / subsidiary in last 2 FYs or current FY
  • Holds shares >2% of paid-up capital (self or relatives)
  • Chief Executive/MD/Partner of any entity that transacts >10% business with the company
  • A practicing CA/CS/CWA/Advocate of the company in preceding 3 years

Code for Independent Directors — Schedule IV (Key Duties)

Safeguard Minority Interests
Balance conflicting interests of stakeholders
Report Concerns
Report unethical behaviour or violation of governance; whistleblower protection
Separate Meeting
IDs must hold at least 1 meeting per year without NEDs/EDs/Management
Performance Evaluation
IDs evaluate Chairman's performance and Board as a whole
⚠ Databank Requirement — Rule 6 of ID Rules
Every individual intending to be an ID must register in the Databank maintained by IICA (Indian Institute of Corporate Affairs) and pass an online proficiency self-assessment test within 2 years of inclusion in databank (unless exempted based on experience — 10 years of service as MD/WTD/ED or 10 years as CS/CA).
Framework 05

The Disqualification & Vacation of Office Framework

Vacation of Office — Sec 167 (Automatic Vacation)

Trigger Event Section Effect Key Nuance
Incurs Disqualification [Sec 164] Sec 167(1)(a) Office vacated immediately Automatic; no further action needed
Absence from ALL Board Meetings Sec 167(1)(b) Vacates office if absent for 12 months (i.e., all meetings in 12-month period) Leave of absence granted by Board does NOT protect; actual attendance required
Acts Prohibited by Section 184 Sec 167(1)(c) Vacates if enters into contract/arrangement where interest disclosed but without Board approval Related-party transaction governance failure
Unsound Mind / Adjudicated Insolvent Sec 167(1)(d) & (e) Automatic vacation Court order required for unsound mind
Convicted & Sentenced ≥6 months Sec 167(1)(f) Vacation from date of conviction Appeal does NOT automatically suspend vacation unless court stays order
Failure to Disclose Interest Sec 167(1)(g) Vacation of office DIR-8 declaration to be filed at each AGM and at first board meeting each FY
🚨 Sec 167(2) — Criminal Liability Post Vacation
If a director continues to act as director after knowing of his vacation, he shall be punishable with imprisonment up to 1 year OR fine ₹1 lakh – ₹5 lakh, or BOTH. This is a critical IBS answer point.

Resignation of Director — Sec 168

⬡ Resignation Process Flow
1
Director gives notice in writing to the company
2
Resignation effective from the date company receives notice OR as specified in notice (whichever is later)
3
Company files Form DIR-12 with ROC within 30 days
4
Director may also file Form DIR-11 (self-filing) with ROC within 30 days of resignation
IF
All directors resign simultaneously
→
Promoter(s) OR Central Government appoints required number of directors to hold office till new directors are appointed by shareholders in GM

Framework 07

IBS Cross-Subject Integration Trigger Map

1. DIN & Appointment Defaults

FR (Financial Reporting) Related-party disclosures (Ind AS 24) require identification of KMPs & directors; invalid appointment → disclosure flag in FS.
Audit CARO 2020 Cl. 17: Auditor to report whether any director is disqualified [Sec 164(2)]. SA 580 — Written Representation from management. SA 250 — Laws & Regulations compliance.
DT (Direct Tax) Director's remuneration → Sec 36(1)(ii) IT Act; disallowance if deemed dividend. Deemed dividend under Sec 2(22)(e) if director (with ≥10% shareholding) receives loan from company.
IDT (GST) Director's sitting fees — RCM liability on company; Director not an employee → services taxable under GST [Entry 6 of Schedule III excludes employees not directors].

2. Independent Director Framework

FR (Financial Reporting) Ind AS 24 KMP disclosure; IDs' remuneration disclosed separately. Audit Committee oversight → materiality judgement in FS preparation.
Audit SA 260: Auditor communicates with Those Charged with Governance (TCWG = Audit Committee chaired by ID). SA 265: Communicating significant deficiencies.
DT Independent Director sitting fees → "Income from Other Sources" or "Business Income" for the ID. TDS under Sec 194J (professional fees) if applicable.
IDT (GST) ID's service is a taxable supply; company pays GST under RCM at 18%. GST credit available to company if registered.

3. Sec 164(2) Disqualification

FR Going-concern assessment; non-filing of FS signals going-concern risk. Consolidation: disqualified director holding directorship in subsidiary — disclosure & governance risk.
Audit CARO 2020, Clause 17: Mandatory reporting if directors are disqualified under Sec 164(2). Qualified/Adverse opinion trigger if Board is non-compliant.
DT Shell/defaulting companies often misuse director positions for tax evasion; GAAR provisions and Sec 68/69 unexplained cash may apply.
IDT Non-compliant companies may have pending GST returns; same directors may be personally liable for GST dues under Sec 89 of CGST Act (joint & several liability).

4. Vacation of Office & Director Liability

FR Change in board composition → event after reporting period (Ind AS 10); disclosure in notes if material. Changes in KMP → disclosure under Ind AS 24.
Audit SA 570: Audit procedures if key directors resign en masse (going concern indicator). Vacancy in Audit Committee → compliance breach → reportable under CARO.
DT Director's liability for company's tax dues: Sec 179 IT Act — director personally liable for tax not recovered from private company (if director was at fault / neglect).
IDT Sec 89 CGST Act: Directors of private company are jointly & severally liable for GST dues if company cannot pay — critical IBC/insolvency scenario.
Framework 08

Critical Compliance Indicators — "Non-Negotiables" for IBS Case Study

  • 01
    DIN Check First: In any appointment scenario, verify DIN validity & KYC status before proceeding. Appointment without valid DIN is void.
  • 02
    Sec 164(2) — Automatic Disqualification: If facts show company has not filed FS/AR for 3 years → all directors automatically disqualified → no valid board → company governance fails. This is the most-tested point.
  • 03
    ID Tenure Math: Two terms × 5 years = 10 years max. If re-appointment is without SR → invalid. Cooling-off = 3 years. Always check whether Ordinary Resolution or Special Resolution is needed.
  • 04
    Residency Rule: Every company must have at least 1 director with ≥182 days Indian residency in previous calendar year. Failure = penalty for company + every officer in default.
  • 05
    20/10 Directorship Cap: In M&A or group restructuring scenarios — check if proposed director already holds 10 public company directorships. Accepting appointment beyond limit attracts daily fine.
  • 06
    Women Director Applicability: Turnover ≥₹300 Cr OR Paid-up capital ≥₹100 Cr (public company). If company crosses threshold mid-year → compliance required within 6 months.
  • 07
    Vacation vs Resignation: Vacation is automatic/operation of law; resignation needs notice + DIR-11/DIR-12 filings. Director continues liable for acts done before vacation.
  • 08
    CARO Clause 17 — Auditor's Weapon: Auditor MUST report disqualification under Sec 164(2). If this is missed → audit quality failure. Always connect Sec 164(2) to CARO reporting in answers.
  • 09
    RCM on Director Services: Director sitting fees / consulting fees → GST liability under Reverse Charge Mechanism on company. Missed by many candidates — score differentiator.
  • 10
    Sec 179 IT Act Link: In a struggling private company scenario, surviving directors may face personal income tax liability for company's tax dues — critical in IBC/turnaround questions.
Framework 09

IRAC Ready-to-Use Answer Templates

Template A: Sec 164(2) Disqualification in IBS Case Study

I
Issue: Whether Mr. X is disqualified from being a director and, if so, what are the consequential implications for the company and its auditor?
R
Rule: Sec 164(2) of the Companies Act 2013 provides that a person shall be disqualified from being appointed as a director if he holds, or has held, office as a director in any company that has failed to file financial statements or annual returns for any continuous period of 3 financial years, or has failed to repay deposits/debentures/interest/dividend declared for a period of ≥1 year. Such person is disqualified for 5 years. Further, CARO 2020, Clause 17 requires the auditor to report whether any director is disqualified under Sec 164(2).
A
Application: In the given facts, ABC Ltd. has not filed its financial statements for [FY 20XX, 20XX+1, 20XX+2] — a continuous period of 3 years. Accordingly, Mr. X, being a director of ABC Ltd., is automatically disqualified under Sec 164(2). His vacation of office in ABC Ltd. and all other directorships he holds is triggered under Sec 167(1)(a). If he continues to act as director despite knowing of the disqualification, he is exposed to imprisonment up to 1 year or fine ₹1–5 lakh under Sec 167(2). The auditor of each company where Mr. X holds directorship is obligated to report this disqualification under CARO 2020.
C
Conclusion: Mr. X is disqualified for 5 years from being a director in any company. His existing directorships stand vacated. The statutory auditor must report the disqualification in the CARO report, and management must disclose this in the Directors' Report under Sec 134.

Template B: Independent Director Re-appointment

I
Issue: Whether the re-appointment of Ms. Y as Independent Director for a second term by passing an Ordinary Resolution is valid?
R
Rule: As per Sec 149(10) and Sec 149(11), an Independent Director may serve for a maximum of 2 consecutive terms of up to 5 years each. Re-appointment for a second term requires a Special Resolution and disclosure in the Board's Report. Ordinary Resolution is insufficient for second-term re-appointment [Sec 149(10) proviso].
A
Application: In the given case, Ms. Y completed her first term of 5 years and the company passed only an Ordinary Resolution for her re-appointment. This does not satisfy the statutory requirement of a Special Resolution. The re-appointment is therefore invalid. Ms. Y's second-term appointment is void ab initio. Acts done by her in the interim may, however, be protected under the de-facto director doctrine, but the company must immediately rectify the position.
C
Conclusion: Re-appointment of an ID for a second term requires a Special Resolution. Passing an Ordinary Resolution renders the appointment invalid. The company must pass a Special Resolution at an EGM/AGM to cure the defect, or must treat Ms. Y as having vacated office and appoint a new ID.

Template C: Director Acting After Disqualification

I
Issue: What is the legal consequence if a director continues to act as director after incurring disqualification under Sec 164?
R
Rule: Sec 167(1)(a) provides that office of director is vacated upon incurring a disqualification under Sec 164. Sec 167(2) criminalises continued acting — imprisonment up to 1 year OR fine ₹1 lakh to ₹5 lakh, or BOTH.
A
Application: The moment Mr. Z's company failed to file financial statements for 3 consecutive years, he was disqualified under Sec 164(2) and his office vacated automatically under Sec 167(1)(a). If Mr. Z signed board resolutions, authorised transactions, or represented the company thereafter, each such act constitutes an offence under Sec 167(2). Third parties dealing with the company in good faith may be protected (ostensible authority), but Mr. Z himself faces criminal prosecution.
C
Conclusion: Mr. Z's continued acts as director post-disqualification are criminal offences. He faces personal criminal liability. The company and Board must immediately reconstitute the board with qualified directors and ratify or void acts performed by the disqualified director depending on their nature.
