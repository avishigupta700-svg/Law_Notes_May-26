Who Can Receive Delegation?

(1) Any committee of directors
(2) Managing Director
(3) Manager / any principal officer
(4) Principal officer of branch office

Cannot be Delegated

Items (a)–(c) and (g)–(j) of §179(3) — buyback, calls, issue of securities, financials approval, M&A, diversification, takeover — always at Board meeting only.

§186(5) — Unanimous Resolution Required

Investments / Loans / Guarantees / Securities under §186 must be passed with consent of ALL directors present (not just majority) + prior PFI approval if term loan subsists.

§180 — Restriction on Borrowing Powers (Special Resolution Required)
Board's Borrowing Limit (without Special Res.) =
Paid-up Share Capital + Free Reserves + Securities Premium
(Exclude: Temporary loans from bankers in ordinary course of business)
🔀 Decision Tree — Does the borrowing need Shareholder Approval?
IF
Total borrowings (existing + proposed) ≤ Paid-up Capital + Free Reserves + Sec. Premium
THEN
Board resolution at duly convened meeting suffices. No shareholder approval needed.
ELSE
Total borrowings exceed the limit
THEN
Special Resolution required in general meeting specifying the total amount Board may borrow.
NOTE
Lender who lends beyond the limit is NOT protected unless they prove good faith + no knowledge of limit breach. [§180(5)]
NOTE
"Temporary Loans" = repayable on demand or within 6 months; includes cash credit, bill discounting. Excludes capital expenditure loans.
NOTE
§180 does NOT apply to Private Companies (notification exemption, subject to no default in filing).

The Director Conflict Management Framework
Disclosure § 184 · Loans to Directors § 185 · Loans & Investment by Company § 186
§ 184–187
§184 — Director Disclosure of Interest
General Disclosure (MBP-1 Form)

When: (1) First meeting as director, (2) First meeting of each FY, (3) First meeting after any change.
Content: All companies/bodies/firms in which interested, including shareholding.
Preserved: 8 years at registered office (custody: CS or authorised person).

Specific Disclosure §184(2)

When: Any contract/arrangement discussed at Board for the first time.
Effect: Director must not participate or vote on that item.
Exception Private Co.: Interested director may participate after disclosure.
Penalty for non-disclosure: ₹1 Lakh. Contract = voidable at company's option.

Exemption from Disclosure [§184(5)(b)]

No disclosure needed if director (or director + any other director together) holds ≤ 2% of paid-up share capital of the counterpart company. (e.g., if director holds 1% → no disclosure needed; if holds 3% → must disclose)

§185 — Loan to Directors (Restrictions)
🔀 Decision Tree — Is This Loan/Guarantee to Director Permitted?
IF
Loan/Guarantee to: Director / Director of holding Co. / Relative of director / Firm where director/relative is partner
THEN
PROHIBITED — §185(1) applies
IF
Loan to a person in whom director is interested (private co. where director is director/member; body corporate with ≥ 25% voting power controlled by director)
THEN
Permitted only with: (a) Special Resolution + full disclosure + (b) Loan used for principal business activities
EXEMPT
Not applicable to: (1) MD/WTD loan as service condition or SR-approved scheme, (2) Ordinary business lending at prevailing government security yield rate, (3) Holding → WOS / WOS guarantee, (4) Guarantee by holding for bank loan to subsidiary, (5) Nidhis (with conditions), (6) Private companies meeting 3 conditions, (7) Govt companies with ministry approval
§185 Penalty (Contravention)

Company: ₹5L–₹25L · Officer: Imprisonment ≤ 6 months OR ₹5L–₹25L · Recipient Director: Imprisonment ≤ 6 months OR ₹5L–₹25L (or both)

YES

§189 Register of Contracts (MBP-4)

Mandatory for ALL companies. Sign at next Board meeting. Open to members at AGM (proxy also). Exceptions from entry: (a) Sale/supply/service contracts ≤ ₹5 Lakh/year, (b) Banking company bill collection in ordinary course.

FR All RPTs must be disclosed per IND AS 24 regardless of §188 compliance. AC approval ≠ exemption from FR disclosure.
AUDIT SA 550 Related Parties — auditor must evaluate completeness of RPT identification. CARO 2020 Cl. 3(xiii): Report transactions with related parties not in compliance with §177 & §188.
DT RPTs at non-arm's length → Transfer Pricing adjustments under §92-§92F IT Act (international); domestic TP u/s 40A(2) for specified domestic transactions
IDT RPT supply between related persons → GST value = open market value (Rule 28 CGST Rules). Leasing of property to related party always taxable even if zero consideration.
Supplementary — §175
CR
Resolution by Circulation Framework
When can the Board bypass a formal meeting?
§ 175
Valid Circular Resolution — Conditions

(1) Draft circulated to all directors (not just majority) at registered Indian address (2) By hand/post/courier/email/fax (3) Majority of entitled directors approve (4) Noted at next Board meeting in minutes

When Circular Resolution MUST become a Meeting

If ≥ 1/3 of total directors request that resolution be decided at a meeting → Chairperson SHALL put it to a Board meeting. (Not discretionary — mandatory.)

Matters CANNOT be passed by Circulation

All §179(3) items (except §186 matters for Sec 8 companies) must be at a duly convened meeting. Circular resolution only works for non-mandatory-meeting items.

High Yield: IBS Case Study Indicators
⚡ Critical Compliance Indicators — Non-Negotiables for Case Studies
POEM / Residency: If board decisions are made via video conference from abroad → flag POEM risk for a foreign-incorporated holding company. Always check where majority board decisions are made.
Quorum + Interested Director combo: Case study will often have 6 directors, 5 interested, 1 non-interested — calculate quorum under §174(3): minimum 2 non-interested. With only 1, meeting cannot proceed for that item.
The "3-year" trap §182: Companies 1–2 years old CANNOT make political contributions. Check incorporation date vs. contribution date in every case.
§186(5) Unanimous vs. §179(3) Majority: Borrowing at §179(3)(d) = simple majority at Board meeting; Investment at §186(5) = unanimous consent of directors present. Critical distinction.
Private Company Exemptions: §180 (borrowing restrictions), §184(2) participation after disclosure, §188 (related member can vote), §174(3) (interested director can participate) — all disapply or modify for qualifying private companies. Always check if it's a private company before applying restrictions.
Voidable (not Void): Contracts under §184(3), §188(3), and §192 are voidable at company's option — not automatically void. The company can choose to ratify. IBS questions may test whether third-party rights are affected.
Charitable contribution math: Always use 3 preceding FY average, not just the last FY. Even if current year is loss-making, if 3-year average > 0, donation is permissible up to 5%.
Transfer Pricing × RPT: In any case study involving intragroup transactions (service fees, interest, royalty), always connect §188 compliance → IND AS 24 disclosure → Transfer Pricing documentation under §92E. This three-layer answer scores full marks.
Ready-to-Use IRAC Template
📋 IRAC Template — §188 Related Party Transaction Approval (Most Tested Scenario)
ISSUE
Whether XYZ Ltd.'s contract to supply goods worth ₹[X] (≥10% of turnover) to ABC Pvt. Ltd. (in which its director holds directorship) constitutes a Related Party Transaction requiring shareholder approval under §188 of the Companies Act, 2013, and whether the transaction, executed without such approval, is valid.
RULE
Under §2(76), ABC Pvt. Ltd. qualifies as a related party (private company where director is a director/member). §188(1) requires Board consent for any RPT not on arm's length in ordinary course; where the value of sale/supply of goods exceeds 10% of turnover, an Ordinary Resolution is mandatorily required per Rule 15(3). Under §188(3), a contract entered without the requisite approval is voidable at the Board's/shareholders' option if not ratified within 3 months.
APPLICATION
The transaction value of ₹[X] constitutes [Y]% of turnover, which exceeds the 10% threshold. The company has not obtained an ordinary resolution. The director involved had an obligation to disclose under §184(2) and absent from the Board discussion. As no ratification occurred within 3 months, the contract is now voidable at the company's option. The director concerned must indemnify the company for any resulting loss. Additionally, IND AS 24 disclosure is required in financial statements irrespective of §188 compliance status.
CONCLUSION
The transaction violates §188(1) read with Rule 15(3). The company may avoid the contract or ratify it in the next general meeting (if within 3 months). The director faces personal liability for losses plus a penalty of ₹25 Lakh (listed) or ₹5 Lakh (unlisted) under §188(5). The auditor must report this non-compliance under CARO 2020, Clause 3(xiii).
