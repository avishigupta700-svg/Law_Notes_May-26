Framework 01
The SEBI Governance & Enforcement Framework SEBI Act, 1992 · Sections 3–34A
Board Composition · S.4

Management Structure

  • 1 Chairman — nominated by Central Government
  • 2 Members — officers from Ministry of CG
  • 1 Member — from RBI
  • 5 Members — nominated by CG; at least 3 whole-time
Term: max 5 years · eligible for reappointment · retirement at 65 years
Termination: 3 months' notice or 3 months' salary in lieu
Key Powers · S.11, 11B, 11C

Regulatory Powers (SCPM Snapshot)

  • Suspend trading of any security on recognized exchange
  • Restrain persons from accessing securities market
  • Attach bank accounts / property for max 90 days
  • Issue Cease & Desist orders (S.11D)
  • Direct disgorgement of wrongful gains → credited to IPEF
  • Levy penalties under S.15A to 15HB after inquiry
  • Powers of a civil court (CPC 1908) for discovery, summons, oaths

⚑ Critical Compliance Indicators — SEBI Act (High-Yield IBS Points)

  • Attachment Rule: Bank accounts attached for max 90 days; must get Special Court confirmation within 90 days, else lapses.
  • Settlement (S.15JB): Once settled, NO appeal lies under S.15T. Settlement amounts (excluding disgorgement) → Consolidated Fund of India.
  • Contravention by Company (S.27): Every person in charge is deemed guilty UNLESS they prove absence of knowledge or due diligence.
  • Recovery (S.28A): SEBI's recovery has precedence over all other claims (S.11B direction defaults).
  • Immunity (S.24B): Granted only on full & true disclosure + CG recommendation. Automatically revoked if conditions breached or false evidence given.

⚑ Critical Compliance Indicators — LODR (High-Yield IBS Points)

  • Maximum Directorships (Reg 17A): Max 7 listed entity directorships. WTD/MD in any listed entity → max 3 independent directorships elsewhere.
  • Committee Membership Limits (Reg 26): Max 10 committee memberships + max 5 chairmanships across all listed + public companies (Audit & SRC counted only).
  • Material Subsidiary (Reg 24): ≥20% of consolidated turnover or net worth → at least 1 ID of listed entity must be on its board; disposal of >50% stake requires Special Resolution.
  • Compliance Officer vacancy must be filled within 3 months; no interim appointment unless compliant with fresh appointment laws.
  • Omnibus Approval for RPT: Valid for 1 year only; max ₹1 Crore per transaction if details not foreseeable; quarterly review mandatory.
Framework 03
The Capital Issuance Framework SEBI (ICDR) Regulations, 2018 — Issue of Capital & Disclosure Requirements
IPO Eligibility · Reg 6

Track Record Route (Route 1)

  • Net Tangible Assets ≥ ₹3 Cr for 3 preceding years (≤50% monetary)
  • Avg Operating Profit ≥ ₹15 Cr for 3 preceding years (profit in each year)
  • Net Worth ≥ ₹1 Cr in each of 3 preceding years
  • If name changed in last year: ≥50% revenue from new activity
IPO Eligibility · Reg 6(2)

QIB Route (Route 2)

  • Does NOT satisfy Route 1 conditions
  • Issue must be through Book Building only
  • Undertakes to allot ≥ 75% of net offer to QIBs
  • Full refund if 75% QIB allotment condition not met
Key IPO Ineligibility: Debarred promoters/directors · Wilful defaulters · Fraudulent borrowers · Fugitive economic offenders

⚑ Critical Compliance Indicators — ICDR (High-Yield IBS Points)

  • Warrant Tenure (Reg 13): Max 18 months; 25% of consideration upfront; if not exercised within 3 months of payment → consideration forfeited.
  • GCP Cap (Reg 7): General Corporate Purposes + unidentified acquisitions ≤ 35% of proceeds; unidentified acquisitions alone ≤ 25%.
  • Interest on Delay (Reg 146): If securities not allotted or refund not made within stipulated time → 15% p.a. interest payable to investors.
  • Minimum Allottees (Reg 145): IPO allotment invalid if prospective allottees < 1,000.
  • Monitoring Agency (Reg 137): Mandatory if issue size (ex-OFS) > ₹100 Crore; quarterly report until 100% utilization.

The Takeover & Substantial Acquisition Framework SEBI (SAST) Regulations, 2011 — Effective Oct 22, 2011
Decision Tree · Reg 3–4 — Open Offer Trigger

When is an Open Offer Mandatory?

CHECK
What is the acquirer's current + proposed combined shareholding (incl. PACs)?
IF
Current holding < 25% AND proposed acquisition would take it to ≥ 25%
THEN
Mandatory Public Announcement of Open Offer (Reg 3(1))
IF
Current holding ≥ 25% but < Max Non-Public Shareholding AND proposes to acquire > 5% in a financial year
THEN
Mandatory Open Offer (Reg 3(2))
IF
Acquirer acquires Control irrespective of shareholding percentage
THEN
Mandatory Open Offer (Reg 4)
NOTE
For IGP-listed companies: 25% threshold replaced with 49%

IBS Cross-Subject Links — SAST
FR Acquisition crossing 20% → Associate, >50% → Subsidiary. Change in consolidation method. Business combination: Ind AS 103. Goodwill recognition on acquisition.
Audit SA 600 — audit of group components post-acquisition. Control change → going concern reconsideration. CARO: change in group structure disclosure. Forensic audit triggers (UPSI).
DT Open offer payments: capital gains for tendering shareholders (Sec 112A if STT paid). Acquirer: cost of acquisition for future gains. IBC-approved resolution plan: SAST exemption.
IDT Transfer of securities: not a supply under GST. Stamp duty on share transfer. Open offer management fees (merchant banker): taxable @ 18% GST.

⚑ Critical Compliance Indicators — SAST (High-Yield IBS Points)

  • PAC Presumption: Holding company + subsidiaries + same management companies are automatically deemed PACs. Rebuttable only with strong contrary evidence.
  • 5% Annual Creeping Acquisition: Gross acquisitions count; intermittent disposals don't reduce the counter. Fresh issue by target → difference in % = acquisition quantity.
  • IBC Exemption: Resolution plan under S.31 IBC is exempt from open offer obligations — key IBS case study point.
  • Voluntary Open Offer (Reg 6): Holder of ≥25% but <Max Non-Public Shareholding can make voluntary offer to acquire further shares — no mandatory trigger.
  • Escrow Account: Full consideration must be in escrow before acquirer can appoint Directors on target's board (after 15 WDs from DPS).
Framework 05
The Insider Trading Prevention Framework SEBI (PIT) Regulations, 2015 — Effective May 15, 2015
Core Concept · Reg 2(1)(n)

What is UPSI? (14-Category Test)

  • Financial results (quarterly/annual)
  • Dividends
  • Change in capital structure
  • Mergers, de-mergers, acquisitions, delistings
  • Change in KMP (other than superannuation); Auditor resignation
  • Change in credit rating (excl. ESG ratings)
  • Fund raising proposed
  • Agreements impacting management/control
  • Fraud/defaults/arrest of KMP, promoter or director
  • Resolution plan / OTS with banks
  • IBC proceedings (winding-up, CIRP admission, resolution plan)
  • Initiation/outcome of forensic audit
  • Regulatory/judicial orders against company
  • Outcome of material litigation/disputes
Decision Tree · Reg 2(1)(g) — Insider Definition

Who is an Insider?

TEST
Is the person a Connected Person?
CP
Connected = any person associated with company in last 6 months who has/expected to have access to UPSI (directors, employees, contractors, bankers, auditors, etc.)
YES
→ Insider by connection
OR
Person is NOT connected but is in possession of UPSI (however obtained)
YES
→ Still Insider (possession-based)
KEY
Onus of proof is on the person levelling the charge; but person who traded while in possession of UPSI must demonstrate innocence

IBS Cross-Subject Links — PIT
FR Material events → prompt disclosure reduces insider trading window. Ind AS 10 — events after reporting period triggering UPSI (e.g., merger announcement). Fair value impact on trading.
Audit Auditor = connected person if in possession of financial results (pre-publication). SA 240 — fraud risk. CARO 2020: reporting on outstanding regulatory orders. Engagement quality check.
DT Penalty under PIT (S.15G): not deductible (illegal payment). Profits from insider trading: taxable as capital gains/business income; disgorged profits reduce net taxable gain.
IDT Securities transactions: exempt from GST. Compliance services (legal/secretarial) for PIT Code of Conduct: taxable @ 18%. SEBI investigation: no GST on penalty payments.

⚑ Critical Compliance Indicators — PIT (High-Yield IBS Points)

  • Connected Person Lookback: Any person associated with the company in last 6 months is presumed connected — includes former employees, ex-advisors.
  • Possession Test: Trading while in possession of UPSI is prohibited even if UPSI did not influence the decision — it is a near-strict liability offense.
  • UPSI Communication Exception: Communication in "ordinary course of business" is permitted. Example: sharing results with statutory auditors before publication is permitted.
  • Structured Digital Database (SDD): Mandatory from Jan 2021; must record all persons with whom UPSI shared; cannot be modified; subject to SEBI inspection.
  • Trading Plan Mechanism: The only "safe harbor" for an insider to trade — pre-approved, publicly disclosed plan with no deviation permitted for 12 months.

Criminal Liability · S.24

Over & Above Civil Penalties

  • Any contravention: imprisonment up to 10 years + fine up to ₹25 Crore or both
  • Failure to pay penalty/comply with directions: imprisonment min 1 month to max 10 years
  • Court cognizance only on SEBI complaint
  • Tried by Special Court (Sessions Judge level)
  • Compoundable offences: can be compounded by SAT/court (except imprisonment-only offences)
Adjudication Framework · S.15-I & 15J

Quantum of Penalty — Factors

  • Amount of disproportionate gain / unfair advantage
  • Amount of loss caused to investors
  • Repetitive nature of the default
Review by SEBI: Can enhance penalty within 3 months of AO order (or disposal of appeal, whichever earlier). Settlement (S.15JB): application-based; order not appealable.

IRAC Template A — Insider Trading (Most High-Yield Topic)
ISSUE
Whether [Name], a [designation/connected person], has violated the SEBI (Prohibition of Insider Trading) Regulations, 2015 by trading in securities of [Company] while possessing Unpublished Price-Sensitive Information (UPSI).
RULE
Under Regulation 2(1)(g), an "insider" includes a connected person or any person in possession of UPSI. Under the SEBI (PIT) Regulations, 2015, no insider shall deal in securities of a listed company when in possession of UPSI. Penalty under Section 15G of the SEBI Act is not less than ₹10 Lakh, extending to the higher of ₹25 Crore or 3× profits. Criminal liability under Section 24 (up to 10 years imprisonment) operates simultaneously.
APPLICATION
[Name] was [describe connection — director/employee/auditor/banker] of [Company] and thus a connected person. The [event — quarterly results / merger / KMP change] constitutes UPSI as it was not generally available and would materially affect the price of securities. The trading occurred on [date], prior to public announcement of the UPSI on [date]. Therefore, [Name] traded while in possession of UPSI, meeting the requisite conditions for violation of PIT Regulations.
CONCLUSION
[Name] has violated SEBI (PIT) Regulations, 2015 and is liable to civil penalty under Section 15G of the SEBI Act and criminal prosecution under Section 24. The company must also review its Code of Conduct and Structured Digital Database (SDD) for adequacy.
IRAC Template B — SAST Open Offer Obligation
ISSUE
Whether [Acquirer] is required to make a Public Announcement of an Open Offer under SEBI (SAST) Regulations, 2011 upon acquiring [X%] shares of [Target Company].
RULE
Under Regulation 3(1) of SAST, any acquirer whose holdings (together with PACs) would equal or exceed 25% of voting rights must make a Public Announcement. Under Regulation 3(2), an acquirer holding ≥25% cannot acquire more than 5% additional voting rights in any financial year without a PA. Penalty for non-disclosure under Section 15H: minimum ₹10 Lakh to higher of ₹25 Crore or 3× profits.
APPLICATION
[Acquirer] currently holds [Y%] shares. The proposed acquisition of [Z%] additional shares would take the aggregate holding (including PACs — [list PACs]) to [X%], which [equals/exceeds] the 25% threshold. Accordingly, Regulation 3(1) is triggered and a Public Announcement of an Open Offer for at least 26% of total shares is mandatory before completing the acquisition.
CONCLUSION
[Acquirer] must make a Public Announcement and comply with open offer requirements under SAST including escrow deposit, appointment of manager to offer, and letter of offer to shareholders. Non-compliance attracts penalty under Section 15H of the SEBI Act.
IRAC Template C — LODR Related Party Transaction Approval
ISSUE
Whether the proposed transaction between [Listed Entity] and [Related Party] requires shareholder approval under SEBI (LODR) Regulations, 2015.
RULE
Under Regulation 23 of SEBI (LODR) Regulations, all RPTs require prior approval of the Audit Committee (only IDs vote). A transaction is "material" if it exceeds the lower of ₹1,000 Crore or 10% of annual consolidated turnover, requiring further Shareholders' Approval by resolution. The related party is prohibited from voting on such resolution.
APPLICATION
[Related Party] holds [X%] in [Listed Entity] [or is part of promoter group], making them a related party under Reg 2(1)(zb). The proposed transaction value is ₹[amount]. The company's consolidated annual turnover is ₹[Y], making 10% = ₹[Z]. Since the transaction value [exceeds / does not exceed] the lower of ₹1,000 Crore and ₹[Z], it [is / is not] a material RPT. [Accordingly, Shareholders' approval is/is not required.]
CONCLUSION
The transaction requires [Audit Committee approval only / Audit Committee approval + Shareholder approval by ordinary resolution]. The related party [name] cannot vote on this resolution. Disclosure must be made to stock exchanges on a half-yearly basis.
IBS Synthesis Note — Multi-Subject Integration

How Securities Law Ripples Across IBS

Scenario: Company announces merger (UPSI) → Promoter acquires 8% more shares → IPO planned within 6 months

  • PIT: Merger announcement = UPSI; promoter acquisition = insider trading
  • SAST: If post-acquisition >5% increase → open offer trigger
  • ICDR: Lock-in of promoter shares; eligibility check for IPO
  • LODR: UPSI must be disclosed via Reg 30 (material event); trading window must be shut

FR / Audit / Tax overlays on the same scenario:

  • FR: Merger → Ind AS 103; promoter acquisition → consolidation reconsideration
  • Audit: SA 240 fraud risk (insider trading); SA 560 subsequent events (merger)
  • DT: Promoter capital gains (STT); UPSI-timed trades → profits potentially disgorged → not deductible
  • IDT: Merger services (due diligence, banker fees) → GST @ 18%; securities exempt
